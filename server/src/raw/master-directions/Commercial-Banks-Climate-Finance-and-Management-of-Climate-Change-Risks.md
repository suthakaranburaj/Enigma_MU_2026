# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Climate Finance and Management of Climate Change Risks) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/172 (DOR.SFG.REC.No.91/30.01.021/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks), corresponding new banks, and the State Bank of India.
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

---

## Summary

These Directions establish a regulatory framework for commercial banks to accept "green deposits" and manage climate-related financial risks. The goal is to encourage the flow of credit to sustainable activities while preventing "greenwashing"—the deceptive marketing of products as environmentally friendly. Banks must implement Board-approved policies and financing frameworks, ensuring that proceeds from green deposits are allocated to specific eligible categories like renewable energy and waste management. The mandate includes annual independent third-party verification and impact assessments to ensure transparency and accountability to depositors.

## Sections

### Chapter I – Preliminary

#### A. Short Title and Commencement
1. These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Climate Finance and Management of Climate Change Risks) Directions, 2025.
2. ⚠️ Obligation: These Directions shall come into force with immediate effect.

#### B. Applicability
3. Applicable to Commercial Banks, including SBI and corresponding new banks, but excluding SFBs, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks.

#### C. Definitions
4. Defines 'green activities/projects', 'green deposit', 'green finance', and 'greenwashing'.

### Chapter II – Role of the Board
6. ⚠️ Obligation: A bank desirous of raising green deposits shall put in place comprehensive Board-approved policies on Green Deposits and Financing Framework.
7. ⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall be responsible for reviewing the bank’s green deposit initiatives through a review report.

### Chapter III – Framework for Acceptance of Green Deposits

#### A. Policy
8. ⚠️ Obligation: The Board-approved policy shall lay down all aspects of issuance and allocation. A copy must be available on the bank's website.
⚠️ Obligation: A bank cannot finance green activities first and raise green deposits thereafter for the same.

#### B. Denomination, interest rates and tenor of deposits
9. ⚠️ Obligation: Green deposits shall only be denominated in Indian Rupees.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are not permitted to offer differential rates of interest on green deposits. Interest must be paid as per agreed terms irrespective of the allocation/utilisation of proceeds.
10. Banks may offer overdraft facilities against green deposits.

#### C. Financing Framework
11. ⚠️ Obligation: The Board-approved Financing Framework (FF) must cover eligible green activities, project evaluation/selection processes, allocation reporting, and temporary allocation particulars.
12. ⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall arrange to carry out an external review of their FF.
13. ⚠️ Obligation: Before implementation, a copy of the FF and the external reviewer's opinion must be on the bank's website.

#### D. Use of Proceeds
14. ⚠️ Obligation: Proceeds must be allocated based on the official Indian green taxonomy (or the interim list provided in the directions).
**Eligible Categories:** Renewable Energy, Energy Efficiency, Clean Transportation, Climate Change Adaptation, Sustainable Water/Waste Management, Pollution Prevention/Control, Green Buildings, Sustainable Land Use/Living Natural Resources, Terrestrial and Aquatic Biodiversity Conservation.
**Exclusions:** 
* Fossil fuel extraction, production, or distribution.
* Nuclear power generation.
* Direct waste incineration.
* Alcohol, weapons, tobacco, gaming, or palm oil industries.
* Biomass using feedstock from protected areas.
* Landfill projects.
* Hydropower plants larger than 25 MW.

#### E. Third-Party Verification / Assurance
15. ⚠️ Obligation: Fund allocation shall be subject to an annual independent third-party verification/assurance. This does not absolve the bank of its responsibility regarding the end-use of funds.

#### F. Impact Assessment
17. ⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall assess annually the impact of green finance activities through an Impact Assessment Report with the assistance of external firms.

#### G. Reporting and Disclosures
18. ⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall place Third-Party Verification and Impact Assessment Reports on their websites.
19. ⚠️ Obligation: A review report must be placed before the Board within three months of the end of the financial year, covering amounts raised, lists of projects, and verification/impact findings.
20. Annual financial statement disclosures must follow the RBI (Commercial Banks - Financial Statements: Presentation and Disclosures) Directions, 2025.

### Chapter IV – Repeal and other provisions
21. Supersedes specific guidelines relating to the framework for acceptance of green deposits as communicated on November 28, 2025.
23. These Directions are in addition to, and not in derogation of, other laws/regulations in force.

## Definitions

- **green activities / projects:** Activities/projects meeting requirements prescribed in paragraph 14.
- **green deposit:** An interest-bearing deposit for a fixed period with proceeds earmarked for green finance.
- **green finance:** Lending to or investing in green activities/projects that contribute to climate risk mitigation, adaptation, resilience, biodiversity, or nature-based solutions.
- **greenwashing:** The practice of marketing products/services as green when they do not meet the required criteria.
