# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/155 (DOR.AUT.REC.No.74/24-01-041/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (including SBI, corresponding new banks, Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Payments
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

## Summary

This Master Direction provides a comprehensive regulatory framework for the issuance and conduct of credit, debit, and co-branded cards by commercial banks in India. It establishes eligibility criteria (minimum ₹100 crore net worth for credit cards), mandates Board-approved policies, and enforces strict consumer protection measures. Key provisions include the prohibition of unsolicited cards, mandatory Key Fact Statements, standardized billing cycles, and rigorous grievance redressal mechanisms, including penalties for delays in account closure.

## Sections

### Chapter I – Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions, 2025.
⚠️ Obligation: These Directions shall come into force with immediate effect.

#### 3. Applicability
These Directions apply to all Commercial Banks, including Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks.

#### 5. Definitions
[Summarized in the Definitions section below]

### Chapter II – Conduct of Credit Card Business

#### 7. Eligibility
⚠️ Obligation: Banks with a net worth of ₹100 crore and above are permitted to undertake credit card business independently or via tie-ups. Setting up separate subsidiaries requires prior RBI approval.

#### 8. Board Approved Policy and Procedures
⚠️ Obligation: Each card-issuer must have a well-documented Board-approved policy available on their website covering interest ceilings, default reporting, co-branding, and grievance redressal.

#### 10. Review by the Audit Committee
⚠️ Obligation: The Audit Committee of the Board shall review credit card operations on a half-yearly basis, including frauds and usage analysis.

#### 11. Customer Acquisition
⚠️ Obligation: Card-issuers shall provide a one-page Key Fact Statement.
⚠️ Obligation: Specific reasons for rejection of an application must be conveyed in writing.
⚠️ Obligation: Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC) must be provided in English, Hindi, and the local language at onboarding.
⚠️ Obligation: Issuing unsolicited cards is strictly prohibited. If billed, issuers must reverse charges and pay a penalty of twice the value.
⚠️ Obligation: If a card is not activated within 30 days, OTP-based consent must be sought. If not received, the account must be closed within 7 working days at no cost.

#### 12. Underwriting Standards
⚠️ Obligation: Issuers must independently assess credit risk and consider limits enjoyed by the cardholder from other entities based on CIC data.
⚠️ Obligation: Transparency in EMI conversion is required; interest components must not be camouflaged as "no-cost EMI."
⚠️ Obligation: Credit limits must not be breached without explicit customer consent.

#### 13-18. Types of Credit Cards
Issuers may offer personal, business, corporate, and add-on cards.
⚠️ Obligation: Liability for add-on cards rests solely with the principal cardholder.

#### 19. Closure of Credit Card
⚠️ Obligation: Closure requests must be honored within seven working days, subject to dues clearance.
⚠️ Obligation: Failure to close within 7 days results in a penalty of ₹500 per calendar day payable to the cardholder.
⚠️ Obligation: Accounts inactive for more than one year must be closed after a 30-day notice period.

#### 22-23. Interest rates and other charges
⚠️ Obligation: Interest rates must be justifiable and governed by a Board-approved ceiling.
⚠️ Obligation: APR must be quoted with clear examples.
⚠️ Obligation: No capitalization of interest on unpaid taxes/levies/charges.
⚠️ Obligation: Minimum Amount Due (MAD) must be calculated to avoid negative amortization.
⚠️ Obligation: Default reporting to CICs and levy of penal charges occur only after an account remains "past due" for more than three days.

#### 24-31. Billing
⚠️ Obligation: Customers must have at least one fortnight (14 days) to pay before interest is charged.
⚠️ Obligation: Protested bills must be explained with evidence within 30 days.
⚠️ Obligation: No charges on disputed "fraud" transactions until resolution.
⚠️ Obligation: Cardholders must be given the option to modify their billing cycle at least once.
⚠️ Obligation: Refunds/failed transactions must be adjusted against "payment due" and notified.

#### 33-34. Issue of unsolicited facilities
⚠️ Obligation: Unsolicited loans/facilities are prohibited without explicit consent. Unilateral limit upgrades are prohibited.

#### 35-36. Reporting to Credit Information Companies
⚠️ Obligation: Default status must be updated with CICs within 30 days of settlement. Pending disputes require caution before reporting default.

#### 37-44. Customer Conduct
⚠️ Obligation: Issuers and agents must adhere to the Fair Practices Code.
⚠️ Obligation: Recovery agents must refrain from intimidation, harassment, or humilitating family members.
⚠️ Obligation: Telemarketers must only call between 10:00 and 19:00 hrs.
⚠️ Obligation: Issuers are liable for the acts of their agents and must blacklist agents for repetitive mis-selling or harassment.

### Chapter III – Issue of Debit Card by Banks

#### 45-48. Issue of Debit Card
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must have a Board-approved debit card policy.
⚠️ Obligation: Debit cards can only be issued to Savings/Current account holders (not cash credit/loan accounts).
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not force customers to avail debit cards or link them to other facilities.

#### 49-50. Review and Policy
⚠️ Obligation: Half-yearly review of debit card operations is required.

### Chapter IV – Form Factor

#### 51-53. Issue of Form Factor
⚠️ Obligation: Wearables or other form factors require explicit customer consent and are subject to the same guidelines as plastic cards.

### Chapter V – Co-branding Arrangement

#### 54-57. Issue of Co-branded Cards
⚠️ Obligation: The co-branding partner must not market the card as its own; the issuer's branding must be prominent.

#### 58-60. Policy and Due Diligence
⚠️ Obligation: Board-approved policy must address reputation risk and revenue sharing.
⚠️ Obligation: Issuers are liable for the acts of the co-branding partner.

#### 61-63. Role of Partner Entity
⚠️ Obligation: The partner's role is limited to marketing and distribution. They shall not have access to transaction information post-issuance.

### Chapter VI – General Guidelines for Credit and Debit Cards

#### 64-73. General Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Internal records must be maintainted for tracing errors.
⚠️ Obligation: Lost cards must be blocked immediately upon information.
⚠️ Obligation: Multiple 24x7 channels must be provided for blocking (helpline, SMS, email, app).
⚠️ Obligation: Renewal cards cannot be dispatched if the customer declines.
⚠️ Obligation: Rewards and discounts must be transparent and verifiable.

#### 74-81. Terms and conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Contractual terms must be expressed clearly in simple language.
⚠️ Obligation: Any change in terms requires 30 days' notice.

#### 83-85. Redressal of grievances
⚠️ Obligation: A Grievance Redressal Mechanism with a designated officer is mandatory.
⚠️ Obligation: Complaints not resolved within 30 days can be escalated to the RBI Ombudsman.

#### 86-88. Confidentiality of customer information
⚠️ Obligation: Explicit consent is required before sharing customer information with third parties.

#### 89. Outsourcing of various services
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must adhere to the RBI Outsourcing Directions. Card data storage/ownership must remain with the issuer.

### Chapter VII – Miscellaneous

#### 91. Contents of the Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC)
⚠️ Obligation: MITC must include fees, drawal limits, billing details, default consequences, termination procedures, and grievance framework.

#### 92. Disclosure of the MITC
⚠️ Obligation: MITC must be disclosed in stages: marketing, application (Key Fact Statement), welcome kit, and billing.
⚠️ Obligation: Font size must be minimum Arial-12.

### Chapter VIII – Repeal and Other Provisions

#### 93-94. Repeal and saving
Existing Directions are repealed. Actions taken under repealed Directions remain valid.

## Definitions

- **Add-on Credit Card:** A supplementary card issued to a relative of the primary cardholder within the same credit limit.
- **Annual Percentage Rate (APR):** The total annual cost of credit, including interest and all other charges.
- **Billing Cycle:** The regular period between two consecutive bill closing dates.
- **Charge Card:** A credit card where the full balance must be paid by the due date (no revolving credit).
- **Co-branded Card:** A card issued jointly by a bank and a non-banking partner entity.
- **Form Factor:** Physical or virtual instruments (like wearables) used in place of a plastic card.
- **MITC:** Most Important Terms and Conditions governing card usage.
- **Unsolicited Credit Card:** A card issued without a specific request or application.
- **Upgradation:** Enhancement of card benefits or increase in credit limits.
