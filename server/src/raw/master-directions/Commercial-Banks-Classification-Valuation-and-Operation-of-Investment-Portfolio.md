# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Classification, Valuation, and Operation of Investment Portfolio) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/162 (DOR.MRG.REC.No.81/00-00-001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (including Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks), corresponding new banks, and the State Bank of India.
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025.

---

## Summary

These Directions establish a comprehensive framework for commercial banks in India regarding the classification, valuation, and operation of their investment portfolios. Banks must adopt a Board-approved Investment Policy and categorize investments into three categories: Held to Maturity (HTM), Available for Sale (AFS), and Fair Value through Profit and Loss (FVTPL), with Held for Trading (HFT) as a sub-category. The guidelines mandate that all investments be initially recognized at fair value and provide detailed procedures for subsequent measurement, reclassification between categories, and provisioning for Non-Performing Investments (NPI).

## Sections

### Chapter I: Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Classification, Valuation, and Operation of Investment Portfolio) Directions, 2025.

#### 2. Effective Date
These Directions shall come into effect from the date of issue.

#### 3. Applicability
⚠️ Obligation: These Directions shall be applicable to Commercial Banks (hereinafter collectively referred to as 'banks' and individually as a 'bank'), including banking companies (other than Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks), corresponding new banks, and the State Bank of India.

#### 4. Definitions
[See Definitions section below for full list]

### Chapter II: Role of the Board

#### 6. Policy Approval
⚠️ Obligation: The Board of Directors (Board) shall approve the Investment Policy of the bank as specified in paragraph 16.

#### 7. Investment Committee
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall constitute an Investment Committee, which shall be responsible and accountable for all investments made by the bank in equity shares, preference shares, convertible instruments, and equity-like products.

#### 8. Impairment Threshold
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall set the threshold for determining the impairment of bank’s investment in subsidiaries, associates or joint ventures.

#### 9. Reclassification Approval
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall provide approval for reclassification of investments between categories (viz., HTM, AFS, and FVTPL).

#### 10. HTM Sale Policy
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall approve the policy governing sale of investments from HTM.

#### 11. Risk Management Oversight
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall be the responsible for ensuring that proper risk management systems are in place for capturing and analysing the risks in respect of non-SLR investments.

#### 12. Quarterly Review
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall also review the various aspects of non-SLR investments at least on quarterly intervals.

#### 13. Broker Limit Breach
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall be post facto informed of the breach of the aggregate upper contract limit for individual brokers.

#### 14. Half-Yearly Review
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall review the bank’s investment portfolio on a half yearly basis (as of March 31st and September 30th). The review shall be placed before the Board within two months from the end of the respective half year.

### Chapter III: General Guidelines

#### A. Investment Policy Framework
#### 15. Compliance
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall undertake investment activities as per the terms and conditions specified in these Directions.

#### 16. Comprehensive Policy
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall adopt a comprehensive Investment Policy duly approved by the Board.

#### 17. Minimum Policy Contents
⚠️ Obligation: The Investment Policy shall, at the minimum, include investment criteria, objectives, authorized securities, derivatives, authority levels, sanction procedures, prudential limits, and broker policies.

#### 18. Sound Business Practices
⚠️ Obligation: The Investment Policy shall be framed to ensure that transactions in securities and derivatives are conducted in accordance with sound and acceptable business practices.

#### 19. Prudential Limits
⚠️ Obligation: The Investment Policy shall lay down prudential limits for investment in securities including those on private placement basis, sub-limits for PSU bonds, corporate bonds, guaranteed bonds, and issuer ceilings.

#### 20. Risk Management Systems
⚠️ Obligation: There shall be proper risk management systems for making investment in corporate bonds which shall include entry-level minimum credit ratings and various concentration limits.

#### 21. Equity Investment Procedures
⚠️ Obligation: Investment policy shall cover in detail the procedure for investment in equities and the policy for managing associated risks. commensurately scaled equity research expertise must be built.

#### 22. Investment Committee Accountability
⚠️ Obligation: The decision to make investment in equity shares, preference shares, convertible instruments, and equity like products shall be taken by the Investment Committee, which will be held accountable.

#### 23. Credit Risk Analysis
⚠️ Obligation: Investment proposals shall be subjected to the same degree of credit risk analysis as any loan proposal.

#### 24. Defaulter Checks
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall refer to the list of defaulters obtained from Credit Information Companies and CRILC while taking investment decisions.

#### 25. Internal Credit Analysis
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall undertake its own internal credit analysis and credit rating and shall not entirely rely on external ratings.

#### 26. Rating Migration Tracking
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall ensure robust internal credit rating systems including regular (quarterly or half-yearly) tracking of the issuer’s financial position.

#### 27. Transaction Settlement
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall settle the transactions in securities and derivatives as per procedure prescribed by the concerned regulator.

#### 28. Dematerialization
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall hold its investments in securities only in dematerialized form.

#### 29. Offshore Branches
⚠️ Obligation: Investment by offshore branches of an Indian bank shall be in accordance with the Board approved policy, including risk perceptions and host country regulations.

#### 30. Primary Dealer Activities
⚠️ Obligation: The Investment Policy shall be suitably framed to also include Primary Dealer (PD) activities where carried out departmentally, adhering to relevant Master Directions.

### Chapter IV: Classification of Investments

#### A. Categorization of investments
#### 33. Three Categories
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall classify its entire investment portfolio (except investments in subsidiaries, JVs, and associates) under Held to Maturity (HTM), Available for Sale (AFS), and Fair Value through Profit and Loss (FVTPL).
⚠️ Obligation: HFT shall be a separate sub-category within FVTPL.
⚠️ Obligation: The category must be decided before or at acquisition and properly documented.

#### 34. Balance Sheet Presentation
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall present investments in the Balance Sheet under six specific heads: (1) Government Securities, (2) Other approved securities, (3) Shares, (4) Debentures & Bonds, (5) Subsidiaries/JVs, (6) Others.

#### B. Held To Maturity (HTM)
#### 35. HTM Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Classification under HTM is permitted only if the security is acquired with the objective to collect contractual cash flows AND the terms meet the Solely Payments of Principal and Interest (SPPI) criterion.

#### 36. SPPI Ineligibility
⚠️ Obligation: Convertible instruments, Basel III AT1/Tier 2 loss-absorbency instruments, non-interest coupons, and all equity/preference shares (except specific AFS election) are ineligible for HTM or AFS.

#### 37. Securitization Notes
⚠️ Obligation: Non-equity tranches of securitization notes meet SPPI only if the tranche terms, underlying pool, and relative credit risk meet specific alignment criteria.

#### C. Available for Sale (AFS)
#### 38. AFS Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Classification under AFS requires an objective of both collecting contractual cash flows and selling securities, and must meet the SPPI criterion.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks may make an irrevocable election at initial recognition to classify non-trading equity instruments under AFS.

#### D. Fair Value through Profit and Loss (FVTPL)
#### 40. FVTPL Scope
⚠️ Obligation: Securities not qualifying for HTM or AFS must be classified under FVTPL.

#### D.1 Held For Trading (HFT)
#### 41. HFT Rules
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must create an HFT sub-category within FVTPL for instruments held for short-term resale, price movement profiting, arbitrage, or hedging such risks.
⚠️ Obligation: HFT instruments must be fair valued daily with changes recognized in P&L.
⚠️ Obligation: Unlisted equities, subsidiary/JV investments, and securitisation warehousing instruments must NOT be in HFT.

### Chapter V: Initial Recognition

#### 43. Fair Value at Recognition
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall measure all investments at fair value on initial recognition. Acquisition cost is presumed to be fair value unless facts suggest otherwise.

#### 44. Government Securities Recognition
⚠️ Obligation: For RBI auctions/OMO, the allotment price shall be the fair value for initial recognition.

#### 45. Day 1 Gain/Loss
⚠️ Obligation: Any Day 1 gain/loss for quoted securities must be recognized in the P&L Account under 'Other Income'.

#### 46. Level 3 Loss
⚠️ Obligation: Any Day 1 loss from Level 3 investments shall be recognized immediately.

#### 47. Level 3 Gain
⚠️ Obligation: Day 1 gains from Level 3 investments must be deferred. Debt instrument gains are amortized over the tenor; unquoted equity gains are held as a liability until listing or derecognition.

### Chapter VI: Subsequent Measurement

#### A. HTM
#### 48. Cost Basis
⚠️ Obligation: HTM securities are carried at cost and not marked to market, but remain subject to IRACP norms.

#### 49. Amortisation
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall amortise any discount or premium over the remaining life of the instrument, reflected in 'Income on Investments'.

#### B. AFS
#### 50. Fair Valuation
⚠️ Obligation: AFS securities must be fair valued at least quarterly. Discounts/premiums must be amortised.

#### 51. AFS-Reserve
⚠️ Obligation: Net valuation gains/losses (after tax) across all AFS performing investments must be credited/debited directly to an 'AFS-Reserve' without routing through P&L.

#### 53. CET 1 Capital
⚠️ Obligation: AFS-Reserve shall be reckoned as part of CET 1 capital. Unrealised gains in AFS-Reserve are not available for dividend distribution.

#### 54. Recycling to P&L
⚠️ Obligation: Upon sale/maturity of AFS debt instruments, accumulated gain/loss in AFS-Reserve must be recognized in P&L under 'Other Income'.

#### 55. AFS Equity Sale
⚠️ Obligation: Gains/losses on sale of AFS-designated equity instruments must be transferred to Capital Reserve, not P&L.

#### C. FVTPL
#### 56. P&L Recognition
⚠️ Obligation: FVTPL securities are fair valued with net gain/loss credited/debited to P&L. HFT must be valued daily; others at least quarterly.

#### D. Investments in Subsidiaries, Associates and Joint Ventures
#### 59. Cost Basis
⚠️ Obligation: These shall be carried at acquisition cost, subject to initial fair value requirements.

#### 64. Impairment Evaluation
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must evaluate these investments for impairment at least quarterly based on specific indicators (default, downgrade, continuous loss, etc.).

#### 65. Independent Valuation
⚠️ Obligation: If impairment is needed, the bank must obtain a valuation from an independent registered valuer and recognize the diminution as an expense in P&L.

### Chapter VII: Reclassifications between categories

#### 66. Permission Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: Reclassification between HTM, AFS, and FVTPL is prohibited without Board approval and prior approval from the Department of Supervision (DoS), RBI.

#### 67. Prospective Application
⚠️ Obligation: Reclassifications must be applied prospectively from the reclassification date.

#### 68. Accounting Treatment
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must follow specific accounting treatments for reclassifications (e.g., HTM to AFS recognizes difference in AFS-Reserve; AFS to HTM reverses AFS-Reserve against fair value).

### Chapter VIII: Sale of investments from HTM

#### 69. Policy and Disclosure
⚠️ Obligation: Sales from HTM must follow a Board-approved policy and be disclosed in the 'Notes to Accounts'.

#### 70. 5% Limit
⚠️ Obligation: Annual sales from HTM shall not exceed 5% of the opening carrying value of the HTM portfolio. Sales beyond this require prior DoS, RBI approval.

#### 71. Limit Exclusions
Specific categories are excluded from the 5% limit: Sales to RBI (OMO/GSAP), GOI/State buybacks, non-SLR call options, Resolution Plan sales, etc.

#### 72. Profit Appropriation
⚠️ Obligation: Profit on HTM sales must be recognized in P&L and then appropriated "below the line" to the 'Capital Reserve Account' (net of taxes/statutory reserve transfers).

### Chapter IX: Fair Value of investments

#### 74. Quoted Securities
⚠️ Obligation: Fair value must be based on prices declared by FBIL. If unavailable, use quotes from recognized exchanges/FIMMDA.

#### 75-77. Unquoted SLR Securities
⚠️ Obligation: T-Bills at carrying cost. G-Secs based on FBIL prices/YTM. Other approved securities at YTM marked up by 25 bps over G-Sec yields.

#### 78-84. Unquoted Non-SLR Securities
⚠️ Obligation: Detailed mark-up requirements based on ratings for debentures, bonds, UDAY bonds, and special securities.
⚠️ Obligation: Unquoted equity fair value is the break-up value from the latest audited balance sheet (not older than 18 months); otherwise value at ₹1.
⚠️ Obligation: AIF units valued at NAV. If valuation is not disclosed as per frequency or is stale (>18 months), value at ₹1.

#### 86. Fair Value Hierarchy
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must categorize the portfolio into Level 1, 2, and 3 hierarchies and disclose in Notes to Accounts effective from FY ending March 31, 2026.

#### 87. Dividend Restriction
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not pay dividends out of net unrealised gains arising from fair valuation of Level 3 investments. Such gains must be deducted from CET 1 capital (subject to specific risk-weight exceptions).

### Chapter X: Operational Guidelines

#### 88. Government Securities Operations
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must adhere to DvP system, SGL account rules, and specific guidelines for Short Sales, When Issued transactions, and STRIPS.

#### 90. Non-SLR Securities
⚠️ Obligation: Investment in unlisted non-SLR securities (carrying amount) shall not exceed 10% of total non-SLR investments as at the end of the previous FY.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not invest in non-SLR securities with original maturity < 1 year (exemptions for CP, CD, NCDs).
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not invest in unrated non-SLR securities (except infra company bonds within 10% ceiling).
⚠️ Obligation: Investment in liquid/short-term debt mutual fund schemes is capped at 10% of bank net worth.

#### 91. Internal Control System
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure clear functional separation between trading, settlement, monitoring/control, and accounting.
⚠️ Obligation: Deal slips must be prepared for every transaction, serially numbered and properly accounted.

#### 92. Broker Engagement
⚠️ Obligation: Role of brokers must be restricted to bringing parties together.
⚠️ Obligation: Settlement must take place directly between counterparties.
⚠️ Obligation: Aggregate transactions through an individual broker must not exceed 5% of total transactions per year. Breaches must be recorded and reported to the Board post-facto.

#### 93. Audit and Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Copy of half-yearly review reports must be forwarded to DoS, RBI by June 15th and Dec 15th.
⚠️ Obligation: Treasury transactions must be subjected to concurrent audit.

#### 94. Reconciliation
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must furnish an annual ‘Statement of the Reconciliation of Investments’ certified by auditors to DoS, RBI within one month of financial year close.

### Chapter XI: Income Recognition, Asset Classification and Provisioning

#### 95-97. Income Recognition
⚠️ Obligation: Accrual basis for G-Secs/Corporate bonds (if interest is regular) and declared dividends. Cash basis for MF/AIF units.

#### 99. NPI Classification
⚠️ Obligation: Criteria for NPA (IRACP norms) apply to NPI. Debt is NPI if interest/instalment is unpaid for > 90 days.
⚠️ Obligation: If a credit facility is NPA, all securities of the same issuer are NPI (and vice versa).

#### 102. NPI Provisioning
⚠️ Obligation: Provision must be the higher of: (1) IRACP provision on carrying value or (2) Depreciation since NPI classification.

#### 105. Investment Fluctuation Reserve (IFR)
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must create an IFR until it reaches at least 2% of the AFS and FVTPL (including HFT) portfolio.
⚠️ Obligation: Annual transfers must be the lower of net profit on sale or net profit for the year less appropriations.

### Chapter XII: Derivatives

#### 109. Accounting Compliance
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must comply with ICAI Guidance Note on Accounting for Derivative Contracts (revised 2021). Derivative assets/liabilities must be separate line items under 'Other Assets'/'Other Liabilities'.

#### 111. Dividend Restriction
⚠️ Obligation: Net unrealised gains on Level 3 derivatives must be deducted from CET 1 capital and are ineligible for dividends.

### Chapter XIII: Repeal and Other Provisions

#### 112. Repeal
Existing instructions relating to investment portfolio classification and valuation are repealed.

## Definitions

- **Active market:** A market where transactions take place with sufficient frequency and volume to provide pricing information on an ongoing basis.
- **Associate:** An enterprise where the investor has significant influence (presumed at 20% or more voting power) but not control.
- **Carrying cost:** Acquisition cost adjusted for discount accrued at the acquisition rate (for zero-coupon instruments).
- **Corporate bonds and debentures:** Debt securities creating/acknowledging indebtedness, excluding G-Secs, security receipts, and securitisation notes.
- **Derecognition:** Removal of a previously recognized financial instrument from the Balance Sheet.
- **Fair value:** The price received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability in an orderly transaction on the measurement date.
- **Level 1 inputs:** Unadjusted quoted prices in active markets for identical instruments.
- **Level 2 inputs:** Inputs other than Level 1 quoted prices that are observable for the asset/liability directly or indirectly.
- **Level 3 inputs:** Unobservable inputs based on assumptions market participants would use.
- **Non-Performing Investment (NPI):** An investment where interest/instalment/maturity proceeds are unpaid for more than 90 days, or other specific indicators apply.
- **Quoted Security:** A security for which market prices are available at authorized exchanges or trading platforms.
- **Rated Security:** A security carrying a current and valid credit rating from a SEBI-registered agency.
- **SPPI criterion:** Solely Payments of Principal and Interest on the principal amount outstanding.
- **Subsidiary:** An enterprise controlled by another (parent) through ownership (>50% voting power) or control over the Board.
