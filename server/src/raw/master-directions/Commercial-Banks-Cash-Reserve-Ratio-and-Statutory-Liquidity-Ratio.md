# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Cash Reserve Ratio and Statutory Liquidity Ratio) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/150 (DOR.RET.REC.69/12-01-001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** 22 January 2026
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (including State Bank of India, corresponding new banks, but excluding Small Finance Banks, Local Area Banks, and Payments Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** Circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

---

## Summary

These Directions mandate the maintenance of Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR) and Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) by all commercial banks in India. Banks are required to maintain a specified percentage of their Net Demand and Time Liabilities (NDTL) as cash balances with the RBI (CRR) and as liquid assets like gold or government securities (SLR). The document provides comprehensive rules for computing NDTL, defines eligible assets, and establishes strict fortnightly and monthly reporting requirements via the CIMS portal. Non-compliance results in significant penal interest charges and potential regulatory action against bank management.

---

## Key Obligations

| # | Obligation | Responsible Entity | Deadline | Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain average daily CRR balance as prescribed (3% - 3.75% of NDTL depending on the period). | All applicable Banks | Fortnightly | Penal interest: Bank Rate + 3% (first day) or + 5% (succeeding days) |
| 2 | Maintain minimum daily CRR balance of at least 90% of the requirement. | All applicable Banks | Daily | Penal interest on the daily shortfall |
| 3 | Maintain SLR assets (Cash, Gold, or Approved Securities) at no less than 18% of NDTL. | All applicable Banks | Daily (Close of business) | Penal interest as per Section 24 of BR Act, 1949 |
| 4 | Submit Form A (Fortnightly CRR Return) through CIMS portal using digital signatures. | All applicable Banks | Within 5 days of the fortnight's end | Fines for officers up to ₹500; potential license cancellation |
| 5 | Submit Form VIII (Monthly SLR Return) through CIMS portal. | All applicable Banks | Before the 20th of the next month | Fines and potential license cancellation |
| 6 | Classify and value SLR securities according to RBI Investment Portfolio Directions. | All applicable Banks | Ongoing | Not specified |
| 7 | Report Standing Deposit Facility (SDF) balances in Form VIII but exclude from Form A. | All applicable Banks | Monthly | Not specified |

## Sections

### Chapter I - Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Cash Reserve Ratio and Statutory Liquidity Ratio) Directions, 2025.
⚠️ Obligation: These Directions shall come into force with immediate effect.

#### 3. Applicability
These Directions apply to all Commercial Banks, including SBI and corresponding new banks, but excluding SFBs, Local Area Banks, and Payments Banks.

#### 4-5. Reporting Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: CRR maintenance shall be reported in Form A.
⚠️ Obligation: SLR maintenance shall be reported in Form VIII.

### Chapter II - Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)

#### 7. Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)
⚠️ Obligation: Every bank shall maintain in India a cash reserve as a percentage of its NDTL as specified by RBI under Section 42(1) of the RBI Act, 1934, and Section 18(1) of the BR Act, 1949.

#### 8. Incremental CRR
⚠️ Obligation: RBI may require scheduled banks to maintain an additional average daily balance calculated with reference to the excess NDTL over a specified date.

#### 9. Maintenance of CRR
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must maintain average daily balances of 3.75%, 3.5%, 3.25%, and 3.0% of NDTL effective from the reporting fortnights beginning Sep 6, Oct 4, Nov 1, and Nov 29, 2025, respectively.

#### 10. Maintenance of Minimum CRR on Daily Basis
⚠️ Obligation: Every scheduled bank shall maintain a daily minimum CRR of not less than 90% of the required CRR on all days during the reporting fortnight.

#### 11-18. Computation of Net Demand and Time Liabilities (NDTL)
⚠️ Obligation: NDTL must include liabilities to the banking system (net of assets) and liabilities to others (deposits, borrowings, misc).
⚠️ Obligation: Loans/borrowings from abroad must be reckoned as 'liabilities to others' and cannot be netted against lending to banks abroad.
⚠️ Obligation: Upper Tier II instruments must be reckoned as liability for NDTL computation.

#### 19. Liabilities not to be included for NDTL computation
Specified liabilities such as Paid-up capital, reserves, Tier 1 borrowings, net income tax provisions, and certain subsidies (e.g., NABARD) do not form part of liabilities for CRR/SLR.

#### 20. Exempted Categories
Scheduled Banks are exempt from CRR on net liabilities to the banking system, ACU Dollar accounts, OBU liabilities, and specific incremental FCNR (B)/NRE deposits mobilised between July and November 2022.

#### 21. CRR Computation
Banks are allowed a lag of one fortnight to maintain CRR based on the NDTL of the last day of the second preceding fortnight.

#### 22. No Interest Payment
RBI does not pay interest on CRR balances maintained by SCBs.

### Chapter III - Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)

#### 24. Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)
⚠️ Obligation: Every bank shall maintain assets in India valued at no less than the percentage specified by RBI (not exceeding 40%) of its demand and time liabilities.

#### 25. SLR - Eligible Assets
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall maintain SLR assets at no less than 18% of NDTL as of the last day of the second preceding fortnight.

#### 26. Marginal Standing Facility (MSF)
Eligible banks can borrow up to 2% of their NDTL under MSF. Default in SLR compliance due to MSF use within 2% of NDTL does not require a specific waiver.

#### 27. SLR and HQLA
Government securities within the mandatory SLR requirement used under MSF or FALLCR are permitted to be reckoned as Level 1 High Quality Liquid Assets (HQLAs) for LCR.

#### 28. SLR Assets
⚠️ Obligation: SLR assets must consist of Cash, Gold (valued at current market price), or Unencumbered SLR securities (Dated G-Secs, T-Bills, SDLs).
⚠️ Obligation: Securities acquired under RBI-LAF or market repo shall not be treated as encumbered for SLR purposes.

### Chapter IV - Procedure for computation of SLR

#### 29. Computation of NDTL for SLR
⚠️ Obligation: Procedure is similar to CRR, but SCBs must include inter-bank term deposits/borrowing liabilities of all maturities in 'Liabilities to the Banking System'.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must include inter-bank assets of term deposits/lending of all maturities in 'Assets with the Banking System'.

#### 30. Classification and Valuation
⚠️ Obligation: Approved securities must be valued in accordance with the Master Direction on Classification, Valuation, and Operation of Investment Portfolio, 2025.

### Chapter V - Reporting

#### 31-33. Fortnightly CRR Return in Form A
⚠️ Obligation: Every scheduled commercial bank must submit Form A at the close of business on the last day of each fortnight within five days.
⚠️ Obligation: Return must include a Memorandum, Annex A (foreign currency items), and Annex B (investments).

#### 34. Reverse Repo Transactions
⚠️ Obligation: Original tenors ≤ 14 days reported under Money at call; tenors > 14 days reported under Advances to banks.

#### 38. CIMS Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must submit single Form A (fortnightly) and Form VIII (monthly) returns electronically on the CIMS portal using digital signatures. Hard copies are not permitted.

#### 39-40. Return in Form VIII (SLR)
⚠️ Obligation: Submit Form VIII before the 20th day of every month showing SLR held on the last day of each fortnight of the preceding month.
⚠️ Obligation: A statement Annex to Form VIII must give daily positions of assets for SLR compliance.

#### 41. Audit
⚠️ Obligation: Statutory Auditors must verify and certify NDTL compilation in statutory returns for the financial year.

### Chapter VI - Penalties

#### 42-45. Penalties for CRR Default
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are liable for penal interest at Bank Rate + 3% (shortfall on one day) or Bank Rate + 5% (shortfall continues on succeeding days).
⚠️ Obligation: RBI may prohibit a defaulting bank from receiving fresh deposits.
⚠️ Obligation: Late submission of returns attracts penalties under Section 42(4) of the RBI Act.

#### 46-48. Penalties for SLR Default
⚠️ Obligation: Failure to maintain SLR on any day results in penal interest under Section 24 of the BR Act, 1949.
⚠️ Obligation: Persistent default may lead to license cancellation.

### Chapter VII - Repeal And Other Provisions

#### 49-50. Repeal and Saving
The existing Directions are repealed but actions taken under them remain valid.

## Definitions

- **Aggregate Deposits:** Aggregation of demand and time deposits.
- **Approved Securities/SLR securities:** Dated G-Secs, T-Bills, State Development Loans (SDLs), and other notified instruments.
- **Assets with Banking System:** Balances with banks, notified financial institutions, and loans/deposits repayable at call or short notice.
- **Average daily balance:** Average of balances held at the close of business on each day of a fortnight.
- **Bank credit in India:** All outstanding loans and advances, including provisions/refinance but excluding technical write-offs.
- **Banking System:** Banks and financial institutions specified under Section 42 of the RBI Act.
- **Cash (for SCBs):** Cash in hand, net balance in current accounts with other SCBs, sub-section (2) of Section 11 BR Act deposits, and excess balances/SDF with RBI.
- **Cash in hand:** Total amount of rupee notes and coins held by bank branches/ATMs/CDMs, including transit cash on books.
- **Corresponding new bank:** Banks constituted under Section 3 of the Banking Companies Acts of 1970 or 1980.
- **Deemed Cash:** Specific components including cash in hand and excess RBI balances allowed for SLR maintenance.
- **Demand Deposit:** Deposit withdrawable on demand (current, demand portion of savings, etc).
- **Demand Liabilities:** Liabilities payable on demand (current deposits, margin against LC/Guarantees, unclaimed deposits, etc).
- **Fortnight:** Period from 1st to 15th or 16th to the last day of the month.
- **Investment in India:** Investment in approved government and other approved securities (encumbered and unencumbered).
- **Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF):** Fixed and variable rate Repo and Reverse Repo operations.
- **Marginal standing facility (MSF):** Facility to avail liquidity support against excess SLR or by dipping into stipulated SLR up to 2% of NDTL.
- **Market borrowing programme:** Domestic rupee loans raised by Govt of India/States managed by RBI.
- **Net balance in current accounts:** As assigned in Section 18 of the BR Act, 1949.
- **Other Demand and Time Liabilities (ODTL):** Accrued interest, bills payable, gold borrowed from abroad, etc.
- **Scheduled Bank:** Bank included in the Second Schedule to the RBI Act, 1934.
- **State Co-operative Bank:** Principal Co-operative Society in a State for financing other societies.
- **Time Deposits:** Deposits other than demand deposits.
- **Time Liabilities:** Liabilities payable otherwise than on demand (Fixed deposits, time portion of savings, staff security deposits, gold deposits).
