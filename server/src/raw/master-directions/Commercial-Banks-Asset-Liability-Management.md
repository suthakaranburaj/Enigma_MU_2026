# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Asset Liability Management) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/163 (DOR.LRG.No.82/13-10-001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

---

## Summary

These Directions mandate that all commercial banks in India establish a robust Asset Liability Management (ALM) framework to manage liquidity and interest rate risks effectively. Banks are required to implement a tiered governance structure, including Board oversight and specialized committees like the Asset Liability Management Committee (ALCO). The framework involves maintaining strict liquidity ratios, specifically the Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) and the Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR), while conducting regular stress tests to ensure resilience against financial shocks.

---

## Key Obligations

| # | Obligation | Responsible Entity | Deadline | Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maintain Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) at a minimum of 100% | All applicable Banks | Ongoing | Not specified |
| 2 | Maintain Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR) at a minimum of 100% | All applicable Banks | Ongoing | Not specified |
| 3 | File domestic Structural Liquidity Statement (SLS) | All applicable Banks | Fortnightly | Not specified |
| 4 | Conduct annual Board review of liquidity risk strategies and policies | Bank Board | Annual | Not specified |
| 5 | Submit LCR and Funding Concentration returns | All applicable Banks | Monthly | Not specified |
| 6 | Limit Inter-bank Liability (IBL) to 200% of Net Worth (unless CRAR is ≥ 11.25%) | All applicable Banks | Ongoing | Not specified |
| 7 | Assign additional 2.5% run-off factor for Internet/Mobile Banking enabled deposits | All applicable Banks | From April 01, 2026 | Not specified |

## Sections

### Chapter I – Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions are the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Asset Liability Management) Directions, 2025, effective from the date of issue.

#### 2. Applicability
⚠️ Obligation: These Directions apply to all Commercial Banks, including the State Bank of India and corresponding new banks, excluding SFBs, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks.

#### 3. Definitions
Definitions for terms including Business Day, Cash in hand, CRR, Govt security, IRR, Intraday Liquidity, MSF, Net Worth, SLR, and Unencumbered are provided in paragraph 4.

### Chapter II – Role of the Board

#### 6-9. Responsibilities of the Board
⚠️ Obligation: The Board of Directors is responsible for overall management of liquidity risk and must decide strategy, policies, and procedures.
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall establish executive level authority for identifying, measuring, and monitoring risk and review the Contingent Funding Plan (CFP).

#### 10-18. Approval of Policies, limits and reviews
⚠️ Obligation: The Board or its Committee must oversee policies and review them at least annually.
⚠️ Obligation: The Board must approve internal prudential limits for mismatches, Earnings at Risk (EaR), and volatility in Market Value of Equity (MVE).
⚠️ Obligation: Any vulnerability observed in stress tests must be reported to the Board for immediate action.

### Chapter III – Liquidity Risk Management

#### 19-20. Introduction
Liquidity is defined as the capacity to fund asset increases and meet obligations at reasonable cost.

#### 21-28. Governance of Liquidity Risk Management
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must set up a governance structure comprising the Board, Risk Management Committee (RMC), Asset Liability Management Committee (ALCO), and ALM Support Group.
⚠️ Obligation: ALCO must decide on maturity profiles, funding mix, and transfer pricing policies.

#### 29-31. Liquidity Risk Management Policy
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must put in place an effective liquidity risk management policy addressing risk tolerance, funding strategies, and stress testing.

#### 32. Strategy for Managing Liquidity Risk
The strategy must be commensurate with the bank's nature, scale, and complexity.

#### 33. Identification
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must identify liquidity risk for each major on and off-balance sheet position and across all active currencies.

#### 34-39. Risk Measurement – Flow Approach
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must adopt the Structural Liquidity Statement (SLS) format for tracking cash flow mismatches.
⚠️ Obligation: Domestic SLS must be prepared daily and reported fortnightly; overseas SLS reported monthly.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must conduct variance analysis at least once in six months to validate behavioral assumptions.

#### 40-41. Risk Measurement – Stock Approach
Banks may use ratios such as Core deposits/Total Assets and Volatile Liabilities/Total Assets to monitor liquidity.

#### 42-43. Risk Monitoring
⚠️ Obligation: Net cumulative negative mismatches in SLS for buckets up to 30 days must not exceed specified percentages (5% for next day, up to 20% for 15-30 days).

#### 44-45. Liquidity Across Currencies
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must have systems for liquidity positions in major active currencies and perform separate analysis for significant currencies.

#### 46-48. Liquidity Risk Tolerance
⚠️ Obligation: The Board must set an explicit risk tolerance defining the level of risk the bank is willing to assume.

#### 49-50. Management Information System (MIS)
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must maintain a reliable MIS providing timely and forward-looking information under normal and stress situations.

#### 51-52. Internal Controls
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure adherence to policies through systems, procedures, and regular independent reviews.

#### 53-54. Monitoring of Liquidity
⚠️ Obligation: Inter-bank Liability (IBL) shall not exceed 200% of Net Worth (300% if CRAR ≥ 11.25%).
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must frame policies to monitor volatile liabilities arising from bulk deposit concentrations.

#### 55-58. Off-balance Sheet Exposures and Contingent Liabilities
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must monitor cash flows from SPVs, derivatives, and guarantees, especially under stress scenarios.

#### 59-60. Collateral Position Management
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must maintain sufficient collateral and have systems to calculate positions in a timely manner.

#### 61-62. Internal Pricing
Banks may implement transfer pricing mechanisms to incorporate liquidity costs/benefits into product pricing.

#### 63-64. Funding Strategy - Diversified Funding
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must establish strategies to diversify funding sources and tenors, avoiding over-reliance on any single source.

#### 65. Intra Group transfers
⚠️ Obligation: Group heads must ensure adequate liquidity at each constituent entity and incorporate constraints on fund transfers.

#### 66-68. Stress Testing
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must integrate stress testing into liquidity governance, conducting regular tests for bank-specific and market-wide scenarios.
⚠️ Obligation: Results must be discussed by ALCO; vulnerabilities must be reported to the Board and RBI's Department of Supervision (DoS).

#### 69-73. Contingency Funding Plan (CFP)
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must formulate a CFP for responding to severe disruptions, including escalation procedures and lead times for tapping funds.
⚠️ Obligation: CFPs must be tested regularly and reviewed by the Board annually.

#### 74-76. Overseas Operations
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must provide guidelines for overseas branches/subsidiaries and report country-wise SLS monthly.
⚠️ Obligation: Long-term resources must not fall below 70% of long-term assets for overseas liquidity management.

### Chapter IV – Intraday Liquidity Management

#### 77-83. Introduction
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must actively manage intraday liquidity positions to meet settlement obligations.

#### 84-85. Sources and Usage
Defines own sources (CRR, SLR, pledged collateral) and other sources (LVPS receipts).

#### 86-95. Monitoring Tools
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must use seven monitoring tools across three categories (A: All banks, B: Correspondent banks, C: Direct participants).
Category A tools include Daily maximum usage and Available liquidity at start of day.

#### 87-88. Stress Scenarios
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must evaluate intraday requirements under stress scenarios like counterparty failure or market-wide stress.

#### 96-99. Scope and Organisational Structure
⚠️ Obligation: Intraday liquidity risk must be managed on a system-by-system basis for each LVPS.

### Chapter V – Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

#### 100-104. Objective and Definition
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must maintain LCR (Stock of HQLA / Total net cash outflows over 30 days) at a minimum of 100% on an ongoing basis.
Banks may drop below 100% during stress but must report immediately to RBI.

#### 105-113. Calculation Principles
⚠️ Obligation: LCR applies to Indian banks at solo and consolidated levels.

#### 114-123. High Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA)
HQLA must be unencumbered, liquid in stress, and possess fundamental characteristics like low risk and ease of valuation.

#### 124-138. Operational Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: HQLA must be under the control of the liquidity management function (e.g., Treasury).
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must periodically monetise a proportion of HQLA to test market access.

#### 139-144. Categories of HQLA
- Level 1 Assets: Cash, excess CRR, Govt securities (subject to LAF/MSF haircuts from April 2026).
- Level 2A Assets: (15% haircut) Marketable securities with 20% risk weight, AA- corporate bonds.
- Level 2B Assets: (50% haircut) BBB- securities, common equity shares (NSE/BSE index), corporate debt (A+ to BBB).

#### 145-146. Pool of Collateral
Guidelines for including unused portions of HQLA-eligible collateral pledged with CCPs.

#### 147-155. Calculation of LCR
⚠️ Obligation: Level 2 assets are capped at 40% of total HQLA; Level 2B assets capped at 15%.

#### 156-161. Total net cash outflows
Formula: Total expected outflows - Min(Total expected inflows; 75% of outflows).

#### 162-185. Cash Outflows
⚠️ Obligation: Detailed run-off rates for retail deposits, wholesale funding, and OBS items.
⚠️ Obligation: From April 1, 2026, Internet/Mobile Banking enabled deposits attract an additional 2.5% run-off factor.

#### 186-193. Cash Inflows
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall include only contractual inflows from fully performing exposures.

#### 194-204. Monitoring Tools and Disclosure
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must use tools like Contractual Maturity Mismatch and Funding Concentration. LCR disclosures must follow specified templates.

### Chapter VI – Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

#### 205-214. Objective and Definition
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must maintain NSFR (Available Stable Funding / Required Stable Funding) at a minimum of 100% on an ongoing basis.

#### 215-225. Available Stable Funding (ASF)
⚠️ Obligation: Categories defined from 100% (Capital) to 0% (Trade date payables).

#### 226-227. Derivatives Liability
Guidelines for marking to market and netting derivative liabilities.

#### 228-241. Required Stable Funding (RSF)
⚠️ Obligation: Categories defined from 0% (Cash/CRR) to 100% (Encumbered assets > 1 year, NPAs, fixed assets).

#### 242. Encumbered Assets
⚠️ Obligation: Assets encumbered for ≥ 1 year receive a 100% RSF factor.

#### 243-250. Secured Financing and Disclosures
Guidelines for repo/reverse repo treatment and disclosure standards.

### Chapter VII – Interest Rate Risk (IRR) Management

#### 251-253. General Instructions
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must measure IRR from an 'earnings perspective' (TGA) and 'economic value perspective' (DGA).

#### 254-255. Earnings Perspective – TGA
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must bucket RSA/RSL and compute Earnings at Risk (EaR).

#### 256-265. Economic Value Perspective – DGA
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must compute Modified Duration Gap (MDG) and sensitivity of Market Value of Equity (MVE).
⚠️ Obligation: A decline in banking book economic value > 20% of MVE due to a 200 bps shock classifies a bank as an "outlier."

#### 266-272. Monitoring IRR
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must prepare Interest Rate Sensitivity (IRS) statements using 10 specified time buckets.

#### 273. Positions in Various Currencies
Guidelines for converting foreign currency IRR positions using FEDAI spot rates.

#### 274-280. IRR Risk Management
⚠️ Obligation: Board/RMC must set internal limits on EaR and MVE volatility.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must adopt periodic model validation and independent audits for IRR systems.

### Chapter VIII – Monitoring and Reporting

#### 281-283. Liquidity Risk
⚠️ Obligation: Domestic SLS prepared daily; Part A1/A2/A3 filed fortnightly; Part B (Overseas) monthly; Part C (Consolidated) quarterly.

#### 284. Basel III Returns
⚠️ Obligation: BLR-1 (LCR), BLR-2 (Funding Concentration), BLR-4, BLR-5, BLR-6 filed monthly; BLR-3 (Unencumbered Assets) and BLR-7 (NSFR) filed quarterly.

#### 285-286. Interest Rate Risk
⚠️ Obligation: IRS Statements under TGA and DGA must be submitted monthly.

### Chapter IX – Repeal and Other Provisions

#### 287-290. General Provisions
Existing Directions are repealed. RBI reserves the right to issue clarifications or interpret provisions.

## Definitions

- **Business Day:** Opening hours of the Large Value Payment System (LVPS) or correspondent banking services.
- **Cash in hand:** Total notes and coins held by bank branches/ATMs/CDMs, including transit cash on books.
- **Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR):** As defined in the CRR/SLR Directions, 2025.
- **Government security:** As assigned in Section 2(f) of the Government Securities Act, 2006.
- **Interest Rate Risk (IRR):** Risk where changes in market interest rates adversely affect a bank’s financial condition.
- **Intraday Liquidity:** Funds accessible during the business day to enable real-time payments.
- **Intraday Liquidity Risk:** Risk of failing to manage intraday liquidity effectively, preventing timely payment obligations.
- **Funding Liquidity Risk:** Risk of being unable to meet expected/unexpected current and future cash flows and collateral needs.
- **Marginal Standing Facility (MSF):** Facility as per RBI press release dated Sept 30, 2025.
- **Market Liquidity Risk:** Risk of being unable to easily offset or eliminate a position at prevailing prices due to market disruption.
- **Modified Duration:** Approximate percentage change in value for a 100-basis point change in interest rates.
- **Net Worth:** Paid-up capital plus Free Reserves (including Share Premium) plus Investment Fluctuation Reserve and P&L credit balance, less debit balance, losses, and intangible assets.
- **Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR):** As defined in the CRR/SLR Directions, 2025.
- **Time-specific obligations:** Obligations that must be settled at a specific time or intraday deadline.
- **Unencumbered:** Free of legal, regulatory, or contractual restrictions on liquidation or transfer.
