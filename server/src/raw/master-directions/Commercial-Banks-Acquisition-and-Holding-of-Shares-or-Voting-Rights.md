# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Acquisition and Holding of Shares or Voting Rights) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/145 (DOR.HOL.REC.64/16.13.100/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks), excluding foreign banks operating through branch or WOS mode.
**Regulatory Topic:** Governance
**Supersedes:** Directions, instructions, and guidelines relating to Acquisition and Holding of Shares or Voting Rights as communicated vide circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025.

---

## Summary

These Directions ensure that the ownership and control of Indian banking companies remain well-diversified and that major shareholders meet 'fit and proper' criteria on a continuing basis. Any person intending to acquire a 'major shareholding' (5% or more of paid-up capital or voting rights) must obtain prior approval from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The directions set strict shareholding limits for promoters (26% post-15 years) and non-promoters (10-15%), mandate a five-year lock-in period for significant acquisitions, and prohibit entities from FATF non-compliant jurisdictions from acquiring major stakes. Banks are required to conduct annual due diligence on major shareholders and report share allotments and encumbrances within tight timelines.

## Sections

### Chapter I- Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Acquisition and Holding of Shares or Voting Rights) Directions, 2025.

#### 2. Effective Date
These directions shall become effective on the day these are placed on the official website of the Reserve Bank.

#### 3. Applicability
These Directions apply to Commercial Banks, excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks. They do not apply to foreign banks operating via branch or WOS mode.

#### 4. Definitions
[See Definitions section below for details.]

### Chapter II- Prior Approval for Acquisition

#### A. Fit and Proper Criteria
6. ⚠️ Obligation: A banking company shall put in place a board-approved ‘fit and proper’ criterion for major shareholders. Criteria include integrity, track record, compliance with tax laws, absence of serious criminal proceedings/convictions, and credibility of source of funds. For acquisitions ≥10%, criteria further include the soundness of future plans for the bank and the impact of shareholder agreements on management.

#### B. Procedure for prior approval
7. ⚠️ Obligation: Any person intending an acquisition resulting in major shareholding must seek previous RBI approval through the PRAVAAH portal.
8. RBI may seek comments from the banking company on the application.
9. ⚠️ Obligation: The Board of the banking company shall deliberate on the acquisition and assess the ‘fit and proper’ status.
10. ⚠️ Obligation: The banking company must furnish comments, board resolution, and Form A1 to RBI within 30 days.
12. ⚠️ Obligation: RBI's decision to accord, deny, or limit the quantum of acquisition is binding on the applicant and the bank.
14. ⚠️ Obligation: If an aggregate holding falls below 5%, the person must seek fresh approval to raise it back to 5% or more.
15. ⚠️ Obligation: Persons from FATF non-compliant jurisdictions are prohibited from major shareholding.

### Chapter III- Continuous Monitoring Arrangements

#### A. Due diligence
17. ⚠️ Obligation: A banking company shall continuously monitor that major shareholders and pending applicants are ‘fit and proper’.
18. ⚠️ Obligation: The bank must:
(1) Establish a mechanism to obtain information on changes in Form A data.
(2) Immediately report to RBI any concern that renders a person not ‘fit and proper’.
(3) Obtain an annual report on Form A changes within one month of the financial year close.
(4) Forward Board comments on ‘fit and proper’ status to RBI by September 30 each year.
20. ⚠️ Obligation: Conduct due diligence on any person acquiring 10% or more of the paid-up equity capital of a major shareholder.

#### B. Detecting violation of Section 12B (1) of the B R Act, 1949
22. ⚠️ Obligation: Establish a monitoring mechanism to ensure major shareholders have prior RBI approval. Report violations immediately.
23. ⚠️ Obligation: Shareholders covered under Section 12B(3) without prior approval cannot exercise voting rights until approval is obtained.
24. ⚠️ Obligation: Even if aggregate holding is <5%, banks must refer to RBI if they suspect methods are used to circumvent statutory requirements.

#### C. Reporting requirements
26. ⚠️ Obligation: Report share allotment details in Form A2 within 14 days.
27. ⚠️ Obligation: Forward details of encumbrance of shares by promoters in Form B to RBI within one working day.
28. ⚠️ Obligation: Report on encumbrance to the Board and RBI within 30 days of the event.

### Chapter IV- Repeal and Other Provisions

29. Repeals previous Acquisition and Holding directions as of November 28, 2025.
31. These Directions are in addition to, and not in derogation of, other laws in force.
32. RBI interpretation of these Directions is final and binding.

### Annex I

#### A. Prior approval for acquisition of shares or voting rights
1. ⚠️ Obligation: Every person intending to be a major shareholder (≥5%) must obtain previous RBI approval.

#### C. Limits on shareholding
10. ⚠️ Obligation: RBI permission is subject to limits:
(1) Non-promoter:
(i) 10% for natural persons, non-financial institutions, or entities connected to Large Industrial Houses.
(ii) 15% for other financial institutions, PSUs, and governments.
(2) Promoter: 26% of paid-up capital/voting rights after 15 years of business.

#### D. Lock-in requirement
14. ⚠️ Obligation: Acquisitions between 10% and 40% are subject to a 5-year lock-in. For acquisitions >40%, the first 40% is locked in for 5 years.
15. ⚠️ Obligation: Locked-in shares shall not be encumbered.

#### E. Ceiling on voting rights
17. ⚠️ Obligation: No shareholder can exercise voting rights on poll in excess of 26% of total voting rights of all shareholders.

## Definitions

- **Acquisition:** Acquiring or agreeing to acquire shares (equity and preference) or voting rights, directly or indirectly.
- **Aggregate Holding:** Total holding (direct or indirect, beneficial or otherwise) by a person along with relatives, associate enterprises, and persons acting in concert.
- **Applicant:** The person making an application under Section 12B of the B R Act, 1949.
- **Control:** As defined in Section 2(27) of the Companies Act, 2013; includes the right to appoint the majority of directors or control management/policy decisions.
- **Encumbrance:** As defined in SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011.
- **Major Shareholding:** Aggregate holding of 5% or more of the paid-up share capital or voting rights.
- **Person:** Includes a natural person, legal person, Private Equity funds, and investment managers.
- **Relative:** As defined in Section 2(77) of the Companies Act, 2013.
- **Significant Beneficial Owner:** As stated in Companies (SBO) Rules, 2018.
