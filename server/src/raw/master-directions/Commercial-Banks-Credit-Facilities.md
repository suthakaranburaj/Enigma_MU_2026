# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Facilities) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/154 (DOR.CRE.REC.73/07-01-001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (including State Bank of India, corresponding new banks, but excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks).
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025.

---

## Summary

These Directions provide a consolidated and harmonized regulatory framework for credit facilities extended by commercial banks in India. They cover a vast range of lending activities including Digital Lending, Microfinance, Project Finance, Real Estate, and Export Credit. The framework emphasizes robust Board-level oversight, stringent transparency requirements (such as Key Fact Statements), and specific prudential limits (like Loan-to-Value ratios for gold loans and indebtedness caps for microfinance). It also incorporates modern safeguards for digital ecosystems, including regulations on Lending Service Providers (LSPs) and Default Loss Guarantees (DLG).

## Sections

### Chapter I - Preliminary
This chapter establishes the short title, immediate commencement, and applicability to all Commercial Banks (excluding SFBs, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks).

### Chapter II - Role of The Board
#### 5. Board-approved credit policy
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall put in place a Board-approved credit policy covering 14 specific areas, including Digital Lending, Gold Loans, Microfinance, Project Finance, Housing Finance, and Export Credit.

### Chapter III - Digital Lending Guidelines
#### 6. Requirements for bank-LSP Arrangements
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must conduct enhanced due diligence on LSPs regarding technical capabilities, data privacy, and past conduct.
⚠️ Obligation: Any outsourcing agreement shall not dilute the bank’s obligations; the bank remains fully liable for all acts/omissions of the LSP.

#### 8-12. Conduct and Customer Protection
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must obtain economic profile data (age, occupation, income) to assess creditworthiness; no automatic credit limit increases allowed.
⚠️ Obligation: Provide a Key Fact Statement (KFS) before contract execution.
⚠️ Obligation: Ensure all loan servicing/repayment is executed directly in the bank's account without third-party pool accounts.
⚠️ Obligation: Provide a minimum 1-day "cooling-off period" for borrowers to exit digital loans.

#### 13-16. Technology and Data Requirement
⚠️ Obligation: Data collection must be need-based with explicit consent and audit trails. Banks must desist from accessing mobile resources like contacts, call logs, or media.
⚠️ Obligation: All data must be stored in servers located within India.

#### 17-18. Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: All digital lending, including short-term or deferred payments over merchant platforms, must be reported to CICs.
⚠️ Obligation: Report all deployed DLAs on the CIMS portal.

#### 19-28. Loss sharing arrangement (DLG)
⚠️ Obligation: Banks may enter DLG arrangements only with LSPs incorporated as companies.
⚠️ Obligation: Total DLG cover on any outstanding portfolio shall not exceed 5% of the amount disbursed.
⚠️ Obligation: DLG must be maintained as Cash, Fixed Deposits with lien, or Bank Guarantees.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must invoke DLG within a maximum overdue period of 120 days.

### Chapter IV - Lending against Gold and Silver Collateral
#### 32. Timeline
⚠️ Obligation: Instructions must be complied with no later than April 1, 2026.

#### 34-35. General Provisions
⚠️ Obligation: Detailed credit assessment is mandatory if total gold loans to a borrower exceed ₹2.5 lakh.
⚠️ Obligation: Top-up loans or renewals are permitted only if the loan is classified as "standard."

#### 36-40. Restrictions
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not grant loans for the purchase of gold (in any form) or for purchasing gold-backed financial assets (ETFs/Mutual Funds).
⚠️ Obligation: Bullet repayment consumption loans are capped at a 12-month tenor.
⚠️ Obligation: Pledged gold ornaments must not exceed 1 kg per borrower; coins capped at 50 grams.

#### 41-45. Valuation and LTV
⚠️ Obligation: Value gold based on the lower of the average 30-day closing price or the previous day's price (IBJA/SEBI).
⚠️ Obligation: Maximum LTV for consumption loans: ≤ ₹2.5L (85%), >₹2.5L to ≤ ₹5L (80%), >₹5L (75%).

### Chapter V - Gold Metal Loans (GML)
#### 51. General Provisions
⚠️ Obligation: Tenor for domestic jewellery manufacturers shall not exceed 180 days.
⚠️ Obligation: GML can be availed only by gold jewellers who are manufacturers; they cannot sell the borrowed gold.

### Chapter VI - Microfinance
#### 61-62. Definition
⚠️ Obligation: Microfinance loans are collateral-free loans to households with annual income up to ₹3,00,000.

#### 63-65. Income Assessment
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must have a Board-approved policy for household income assessment and must mandatorily report this data to CICs.

#### 66-69. Limit on Repayment Obligations
⚠️ Obligation: Monthly loan repayment outflows (including principal and interest) are capped at 50% of monthly household income.

### Chapter VII - Project Finance
#### 72. Applicability
Directions apply to projects achieving financial closure after October 1, 2025.

#### 75-79. Sanction Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Original DCCO must be documented prior to disbursement.
⚠️ Obligation: Repayment tenor (including moratorium) shall not exceed 85% of the project's economic life.
⚠️ Obligation: For under-construction projects, no individual bank in a group shall have exposure less than 10% (for projects up to ₹1,500 crore) or 5%/₹150 crore (for projects >₹1,500 crore).

#### 80-83. Disbursement and Monitoring
⚠️ Obligation: Minimum land/right-of-way required before disbursement: 50% for PPP infrastructure; 75% for others.
⚠️ Obligation: Disbursal must be proportionate to stages of completion as certified by an independent engineer.

#### 85-87. Database and Disclosure
⚠️ Obligation: Maintain an electronic database for project finance and update changes within 15 days.

### Chapter VIII - Credit Facilities to Real Estate Sector
#### 88-92. Housing Finance
⚠️ Obligation: Board-approved policy must set ceilings on total real estate loans and exposure limits.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must obtain a copy of the sanctioned plan and an affidavit-cum-undertaking before sanctioning home loans to prevent unauthorized construction.
⚠️ Obligation: Architects must certify the property is as per the sanctioned plan before final disbursement.

#### 96. Exclusions
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not grant finance for buildings meant purely for Government/Semi-Government offices (unless refinanced by NABARD).

#### 111-113. Quantum and LTV
⚠️ Obligation: Standard LTV/Risk Weight table (e.g., loans up to ₹30 lakh at ≤80% LTV attract 35% risk weight).

#### 114-118. Innovative Products
⚠️ Obligation: Disbursal of loans for individuals must be linked to construction stages; upfront lump-sum disbursals to builders are prohibited for incomplete projects.

#### 119. Disclosure Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must stipulate that builders disclose the bank's mortgage interest in all brochures and advertisements.

### Chapter IX - Infrastructure Financing
#### 134-137. Criteria
⚠️ Obligation: Term loans to public sector units allowed only for corporate entities, not as a substitute for budgetary resources.
⚠️ Obligation: Bank finance for promoters' contribution (equity) is restricted to 50% of the required finance and subject to a 7-year tenor cap.

#### 137A. Lending to InvITs
⚠️ Obligation: Permitted only if none of the underlying SPVs are in "financial difficulty."

### Chapter X - Discounting / Rediscounting of Bills
#### 146-152. Guidelines
⚠️ Obligation: Purchase/discount bills only for genuine commercial transactions of constituent borrowers.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not purchase/discount "accommodation bills."

### Chapter XI - Acquisition Finance
#### 158. PSU Disinvestments
⚠️ Obligation: Bank finance allowed only for prospective disinvestments and must be approved by the Board.

### Chapter XII - Overseas Joint Ventures (JV)
#### 171-172. Credit Facilities
⚠️ Obligation: Fund-based facilities allowed only if the Indian company holds >51%.
⚠️ Obligation: Total exposure capped at 20% of the bank's unimpaired capital funds.
⚠️ Obligation: Additional 2% provision required for exposures to step-down subsidiaries.

### Chapter XIII - Loans Against Financial Assets
#### 177-178. Individuals
⚠️ Obligation: Loans against shares capped at ₹10 lakh (physical form) and ₹20 lakh (dematerialized).
⚠️ Obligation: Minimum margin of 50% for physical shares; 25% for dematerialized.

#### 180-186. Brokers
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not undertake "Badla" financing.
⚠️ Obligation: Advances only to brokers registered with SEBI.

#### 190. IPOs
⚠️ Obligation: Loans to individuals for IPOs capped at ₹10 lakh.

### Chapter XIV - Finance to NBFCs
#### 224. Ineligible Activities
⚠️ Obligation: Banks cannot finance NBFCs for: bills rediscounting (with exceptions), investments in other companies, or unsecured loans/inter-corporate deposits.

### Chapter XV - Export Credit
#### 235-243. Packing Credit
⚠️ Obligation: Pre-shipment advances cease to qualify as export credit if not adjusted within 360 days.
⚠️ Obligation: "Running Account" facility allowed only for exporters with good track records.

### Chapter XVI - Non-Fund Based (NFB) Credit Facilities
#### 399-401. General Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Effective date April 1, 2026.
⚠️ Obligation: Issue NFB facilities only for customers already having funded credit facilities (with specific exemptions).

#### 404-413. Guarantees
⚠️ Obligation: Guarantees must be irrevocable, unconditional, and incontrovertible.
⚠️ Obligation: Electronic guarantees must utilize a "Maker, Checker, Authorizer" system and STP mode.

#### 422-436. Partial Credit Enhancement (PCE)
⚠️ Obligation: PCE restricted to 20% of bond issue size for an individual bank (50% aggregate).
⚠️ Obligation: Restricted to bonds with pre-enhanced rating of "BBB-" or better.
⚠️ Obligation: PCE is a non-funded irrevocable contingent line of credit; it cannot be a guarantee.

## Definitions

- **Actual DCCO:** The date a project is put to commercial use and completion/occupancy certificates are issued.
- **Credit Event:** In project finance, includes default, need for DCCO extension, expiry of DCCO, or financial difficulty.
- **Default Loss Guarantee (DLG):** A contractual arrangement where an entity guarantees to compensate a bank for losses up to a specified percentage of a loan portfolio.
- **Digital Lending:** Remote/automated lending using digital technologies for the entire cycle from acquisition to recovery.
- **Lending Service Provider (LSP):** An agent of the bank carrying out digital lending functions like customer acquisition or recovery.
- **Microfinance Loan:** A collateral-free loan to a household with annual income up to ₹3,00,000.
- **Project Finance:** Funding where revenues from the project serve as primary security and source of repayment (at least 51% of cash flows).
- **Unsecured Exposure:** Exposure where realisable security value is ≤ 10% ab-initio.
