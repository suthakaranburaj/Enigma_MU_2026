# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Concentration Risk Management) Directions, 2025 (Updated as on January 01, 2026)

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/158
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** 01 January 2026
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (including State Bank of India, corresponding new banks, and foreign banks; excluding SFBs, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

---

## Summary

These Directions establish the framework for managing concentration risk within the Indian banking sector. The primary objective is to prevent excessive exposure to single or connected counterparties, specific sectors, and group entities, which could jeopardize a bank's solvency during financial stress. The directions mandate a Large Exposures Framework (LEF) aligned with global Basel standards, set specific regulatory ceilings for capital market and intra-group exposures, and require robust Board-level oversight and reporting systems to identify and rectify limit breaches.

## Sections

### Chapter I - Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Concentration Risk Management) Directions, 2025.
⚠️ Obligation: These Directions shall come into effect immediately upon issuance.

#### 3. Applicability
These Directions apply to all Commercial Banks, including SBI and foreign banks operating in India, excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks.

#### 4. Definitions
[See Definitions section below]

### Chapter II – Role of the Board

#### 6. Board Policies and Limits
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall have Board-approved policies on Concentration Risk Management covering single counterparties, interconnected groups, specific sectors, and ultra-large borrowers.
⚠️ Obligation: The Board must approve specific policies including:
(i) Additional 5% exposure for single counterparties in exceptional cases.
(ii) Criteria for determining connectedness.
(iii) Sectoral commitment limits (e.g., textiles, jute).
(iv) Unsecured consumer credit sub-segment limits.
(v) Intra-day capital market exposure limits.
(vi) Call/notice/term money market lending limits within LEF.
(vii) Management of intra-group transactions and exposures (ITEs).
(viii) Country and regional exposure limits.

#### 6(2). Review and Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Material ITEs and dealings with group entities inconsistent with third-party benchmarks must be reported to the Board. An annual review of exposure management must be completed before the end of June.

### Chapter III - Large Exposures Framework (LEF)

#### 7. Scope of Application
⚠️ Obligation: LEF shall be applied at both the Consolidated (Group) level and the Standalone (Solo) level.

#### 8. Scope of Counterparties and Exemptions
The following exposures are exempted from LEF:
(1) GOI and State Governments with 0% Risk Weight.
(2) RBI.
(3) GOI guaranteed exposures.
(4) Intra-day interbank exposures.
(5) Borrowers authorized for food credit.
(6) Clearing activities with Qualifying Central Counterparties (QCCPs).
(7) Specified contributions to NABARD, NHB, SIDBI, MUDRA.

#### 13. Bills discounted under Letter of Credit (LC)
⚠️ Obligation: Bills purchased/negotiated under LC (not 'under reserve') where the discounting bank and issuing bank are different shall be treated as exposure to the LC issuing bank.

#### 14. Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall report to the RBI (DoS, CO) using Annex II:
(1) All exposures ≥ 10% of eligible capital.
(2) All exposures ≥ 10% of eligible capital measured without CRM.
(3) Exempted exposures ≥ 10% of eligible capital.
(4) The 20 largest exposures irrespective of value.

#### 15. Single Counterparty Limit
⚠️ Obligation: Exposure to a single counterparty shall not exceed 20% of the eligible capital base. Boards may allow an additional 5% in exceptional cases.

#### 16. Group of Connected Counterparties Limit
⚠️ Obligation: Exposure to a group of connected counterparties shall not exceed 25% of the eligible capital base.

#### 18. Breaches
⚠️ Obligation: Any breach of LE limits shall be reported to RBI (DoS, CO) immediately and rapidly rectified.

#### 20-31. Definition of Connected Counterparties
⚠️ Obligation: Two or more persons constitute a group if they meet:
(1) Control relationship criteria (e.g., owning > 50% voting rights).
(2) Economic interdependence criteria (e.g., one party handles > 50% of the other's gross receipts/expenditures).
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must use a "Look-Through Approach" (LTA) for exposures to structures like mutual funds or securitizations where any underlying exposure is ≥ 0.25% of the capital base.

#### 43. Indian branches of foreign banks
⚠️ Obligation: Foreign bank branches may use interest-free funds from Head Office or remittable surplus held with RBI as CRM to offset exposure to the Head Office, subject to auditor certification and annual undertakings.

#### 46-47. CRM Providers
⚠️ Obligation: If a bank reduces exposure via CRM, it must recognize a corresponding exposure to the CRM provider.

#### 48. Trading Book Positions
⚠️ Obligation: Trading book exposures must be added to banking book exposures for counterparty limit calculation. Market value shall be used for debt and equity.

#### 62. Interbank Exposures
⚠️ Obligation: Non-intra-day interbank exposures are subject to a 25% Tier 1 capital limit.

#### 79-81. NBFC Exposures
⚠️ Obligation: Single NBFC limit is 20% of eligible capital (25% if funds are on-lent to infrastructure). NBFCs predominantly in gold loans are limited to 7.5%. Connected NBFC groups are limited to 25% of Tier 1 capital.

#### 82-84. G-SIBs and D-SIBs
⚠️ Obligation: Exposure between two G-SIBs is capped at 15% of the capital base. Exposure of a non-G-SIB to a G-SIB is capped at 20%.

### Chapter V - Exposure Norms

#### 90. Consumer Credit
⚠️ Obligation: Boards must prescribe limits for all unsecured consumer credit exposures, monitored by the Risk Management Committee.

#### 95. Capital Market Exposure (CME) Components
Defines CME to include direct investment in equity, advances against shares, loans to stockbrokers, and Irrevocable Payment Commitments (IPCs).

#### 96-98. CME Limits
⚠️ Obligation: Solo Basis: Aggregate CME ≤ 40% of net worth; Direct CME ≤ 20% of net worth.
⚠️ Obligation: Consolidated Basis: Aggregate CME ≤ 40% of consolidated net worth; Direct CME ≤ 20% of consolidated net worth.

#### 104. IPCs
⚠️ Obligation: Only custodian banks with specific inalienable rights over securities in client agreements may issue IPCs in favour of Stock Exchanges.

#### 108. Overseas Subsidiaries
⚠️ Obligation: Exposure to Indian JVs/WOS abroad and overseas step-down subsidiaries is limited to 20% of the bank's unimpaired capital funds.

#### 112-113. Safety Net and Buy Back
⚠️ Obligation: Banks/subsidiaries shall not offer "Safety Net" facilities for public issues. Buyback arrangements for small investors must not commit to pre-determined prices and must not exceed 25% of owned funds.

### Chapter VI - Prudential Limits on Intra-Group Transactions and Exposure

#### 116. Intra-group limits
⚠️ Obligation: 
Single Entity: 5% of Paid-up Capital and Reserves (non-financial/unregulated) or 10% (regulated financial).
Aggregate Group: 10% (non-financial/unregulated) or 20% (total group).

#### 118. NOFHC Prohibitions
⚠️ Obligation: Banks under a NOFHC structure cannot take any credit/investment exposure to the NOFHC or its promoter group.

#### 119-120. ITE Policies
⚠️ Obligation: Boards must approve a comprehensive ITE policy, reviewed annually, ensuring arm’s length principles and transparency.

#### 122. Cross-Default
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not enter into cross-default clauses where a group entity's default triggers a bank default.

#### 128. ITE Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must submit a list of group entities and details of support arrangements to RBI. Breaches require immediate reporting and may result in deductions from CET1 capital if not rectified within timelines.

### Chapter VII – Management of Country Risk Exposure

#### 130-131. Country Risk Limits
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall set country exposure limits relative to regulatory capital (Tier 1 + Tier 2). Limits for any country shall not exceed 100% of regulatory capital (except for 'insignificant' risk categories).

#### 135. Indirect Country Risk
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must reckon indirect country risk (e.g., a borrower dependent on a specific country) if net funded exposure to that country is ≥ 1% of total assets.

### Chapter VIII - Prudential Limits for Inter-Bank Liabilities (IBL)

## Definitions

- **Eligible capital base:** The effective amount of Tier 1 capital as per the last audited balance sheet.
- **Exposure (Intra-group):** Includes funded and non-funded credit limits and investment exposure (higher of sanctioned or outstanding).
- **Group (Intra-group):** An arrangement involving entities related via subsidiary, associate, joint venture, or related party status.
- **Large Exposure (LE):** Sum of all exposure values to a counterparty/group ≥ 10% of the bank's eligible capital base.
- **Net worth:** Paid-up capital plus Free Reserves (including Share Premium) plus Investment Fluctuation Reserve and P&L credit balance, minus debit balance, losses, and intangible assets.
- **Qualifying Central Counterparty (QCCP):** A licensed CCP prudentially supervised in a jurisdiction consistent with CPSS-IOSCO principles.
