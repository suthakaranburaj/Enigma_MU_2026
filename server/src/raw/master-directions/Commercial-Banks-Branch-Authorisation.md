# Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Branch Authorisation) Directions, 2025

**Document Type:** Master Direction
**Reference Number:** RBI/DOR/2025-26/147 (DOR.LIC.REC.No.66/22-01-001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 28 November 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** Commercial Banks (excluding Small Finance Banks, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks), State Bank of India, and Wholly Owned Subsidiaries (WOS) of Foreign Banks.
**Regulatory Topic:** Governance
**Supersedes:** circular DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26 dated November 28, 2025

---

## Summary

These Directions provide a comprehensive framework for the authorisation, opening, shifting, and closure of banking outlets and specialized units like Digital Banking Units (DBUs). The policy grants general permission to domestic scheduled commercial banks to open outlets in Tier 1 to Tier 6 centres, provided at least 25% of new outlets in a financial year are located in Unbanked Rural Centres (URCs). The Directions also detail the Business Correspondent (BC) model, doorstep banking services, and mandatory reporting requirements through the Central Information System for Banking Infrastructure (CISBI).

---

## Key Obligations

| # | Obligation | Responsible Entity | Deadline | Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | At least 25% of total Banking Outlets opened in a financial year must be in Unbanked Rural Centres (URCs). | All applicable Banks | Every Financial Year | Restricted opening of Tier 1 outlets |
| 2 | Put in place separate Board-approved policies for opening/closing outlets, BC engagement, DBUs, and Doorstep Banking. | All applicable Banks | Immediate | Not specified |
| 3 | Report opening, closure, merger, shifting, or conversion of outlets/ATMs on the CISBI portal. | All applicable Banks | Within one week of the event | Not specified |
| 4 | Submit a "NIL Report" through CISBI if no changes occurred in the reference month. | All applicable Banks | By the 7th of the next month | Not specified |
| 5 | Carry out a detailed annual review of the performance of engaged Business Correspondents (BCs). | All applicable Banks | Annually | Not specified |
| 6 | Foreign banks must bring assigned capital of US$25 million upfront for the first branch. | Foreign Banks (branch mode) | At time of opening first branch | Not specified |
| 7 | Ensure DBUs source more than 51% of customers and business from the specific centre of operation. | All applicable Banks | Ongoing | Not specified |

## Sections

### Chapter I – Preliminary

#### 1. Short Title and Commencement
These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks - Branch Authorisation) Directions, 2025.
⚠️ Obligation: These directions shall come into force with immediate effect.

#### 3. Applicability
These Directions shall be applicable to Commercial Banks (banking companies other than SFBs, Payment Banks, and Local Area Banks). Paragraphs 28 to 39 apply specifically to Foreign Banks.

#### 4. Definitions
This section defines terms including Administrative Office, Back Office, Banking Outlet, Part-time Banking Outlet, Digital Banking, Digital Banking Unit (DBU), and Unbanked Rural Centre (URC).

### Chapter II – Role of Board of Directors

#### A. Board Approved Policy
#### 6. Policy requirements
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall put in place separate Board-approved policies for:
(i) Opening, merging, shifting, conversion and closure of Banking Outlets.
(ii) Opening of Mobile Branches, Administrative Offices, Back Offices, Call Centres, ATMs, CDMs/BNAMs.
(iii) Acquisition of accommodation on lease/rental basis.
(iv) Opening and operating of Digital Banking Units.
(v) Engagement of Business Correspondents (BCs) and oversight.
(vi) Offering Doorstep Banking services and agent selection.

#### B. Key responsibilities
#### 7. Board Oversight
⚠️ Obligation: The Board shall:
(1)(i) Ensure regular off-site and on-site monitoring of Banking Outlets for uninterrupted service.
(1)(iii) Set internal targets for financial inclusion and review progress quarterly.
(2)(i) Ensure regular monitoring systems for DBUs.
(3)(iii) Review BC operations at least every six months.
(4)(i) Review doorstep banking operations half-yearly in the first year and annually thereafter.

### Chapter III – Banking Outlet Authorisation

#### A. Domestic Commercial Banks
#### 8. General Permission
Domestic scheduled commercial banks are permitted to open Banking Outlets in Tier 1 to Tier 6 centres without prior RBI permission, subject to specific restrictions.

#### 9. Conditions for Opening Outlets
⚠️ Obligation: (i) At least 25 percent of the total number of ‘Banking Outlets’ opened during a financial year shall be opened in Unbanked Rural Centres (URCs).
⚠️ Obligation: (vi) If a bank fails to adhere to the 25% URC requirement, penal measures including restrictions on Tier 1 openings may be imposed.

#### 12. Government Business
⚠️ Obligation: If a bank proposes to undertake government business at any Banking Outlet, it requires prior approval from the Government authority and RBI (Department of Government and Bank Accounts) via the Pravaah portal.

#### 13. Wholly Owned Subsidiaries (WOS)
WOS of foreign banks follow domestic bank guidelines but require prior RBI authorization for branches in locations sensitive to national security.

#### A.2 Merger / closure / shifting / conversion
#### 14-17. Discretion and Restrictions
⚠️ Obligation: Shifting, merger, or closure of any rural or sole semi-urban Banking Outlet requires approval from the District Consultative Committee (DCC) / District Level Review Committee (DLRC).
⚠️ Obligation: Customers must be informed well in time of any merger/closure/shifting.
⚠️ Obligation: Outlets must be shifted within the same or to a lower population category.

#### A.3 Opening of banking outlets – prior authorisation
#### 18-21. Authorization Process
⚠️ Obligation: Banks from which general permission is withdrawn must obtain prior authorisation from the Department of Regulation (DoR, CO), RBI.
⚠️ Obligation: Such banks must submit an Annual Banking Outlet Expansion Plan (ABOEP) as per Annex I.
⚠️ Obligation: The validity of any authorisation granted shall be one year.

#### A.5 Mobile Branches
#### 23. Operation
Banks can operate mobile branches in all centres, but these are not reckoned as ‘Banking Outlets’.

#### A.6 Administrative Offices, Back Offices, and Call Centres
#### 24-25. Setup
⚠️ Obligation: Back offices must not have any direct interface with customers.

#### A.7 Acquisition of Accommodation
#### 26-27. Norms
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure Banking Outlets do not function from premises unauthorised in law and comply with local norms (Municipal/Panchayat).

#### B. Foreign Banks (operating in branch mode)
#### 28-30. Opening of Branches
⚠️ Obligation: Foreign banks must submit an Annual Branch Expansion Plan to DoR, CO.
⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall bring an assigned capital of US$25 million upfront at the time of opening the first branch in India.

#### B.2 Shifting of branches
#### 31-33. Revenue Centre Shifting
⚠️ Obligation: Shifting a sole rural branch outside the centre is not permitted unless under exceptional circumstances with DCC approval.

#### B.3 Part-shifting of branches
#### 34. Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Part-shifting (shifting some activities) is subject to: (iii) the new location being within 250 meters of the existing location; (vi) the same activity not being carried out at both locations.

#### B.4 Merger / closure / conversion
#### 35-37. Rules
⚠️ Obligation: Closure or merger of a sole rural/semi-urban branch is generally not permitted.

#### C. ATMs, CDMs, and BNAMs
#### 40-47. Installation and Management
⚠️ Obligation: Off-site ATM transactions must be recorded in the books of the respective base branch or Centralised Data Centre.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure only properly sorted and examined notes are put into circulation through ATMs.
⚠️ Obligation: CDMs/BNAMs shall not return suspect or counterfeit notes to the customer.

### Chapter IV – Digital Banking Units (DBUs)

#### A. Opening of DBUs
#### 48-49. General Permission
⚠️ Obligation: Banks with past digital banking experience can open DBUs in Tier 1 to Tier 6 centres.
⚠️ Obligation: To be counted as an outlet in a centre, the DBU must source >51% of customers and business from that centre.

#### B. Infrastructure and Resources
#### 50-56. Housing and Governance
⚠️ Obligation: Each DBU must be housed distinctly with separate entry/exit from any existing Banking Outlet.
⚠️ Obligation: A DBU shall be headed by an officer with sufficient qualification and experience in digital banking.

#### D. Products and Services
#### 58-61. Minimum Bouquet
⚠️ Obligation: DBUs must offer minimum products including account opening (Saving/Current/FD), digital kits (Mobile/Internet banking/POS), and digital services (KYC updates, grievance lodging, cash withdrawal/deposit via ATM/CDM).

#### G. Customer Grievance Redressal
#### 64. Mechanism
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure digital mechanisms offer real-time assistance for DBU-related grievances.

### Chapter V – Business Facilitator / Business Correspondent Model

#### C. Guidelines for engaging Business Correspondents (BCs)
#### 69. Due Diligence
⚠️ Obligation: Due diligence on BCs must cover reputation, financial soundness, and cash handling ability.

#### 70. Base Branch Attachment
⚠️ Obligation: Every retail outlet/sub-agent of a BC shall be attached to a specific Banking Outlet manned by bank employees (Base Banking Outlet).

#### 71. Eligible entities
Lists eligible individuals (retired employees, kirana shop owners, PCO operators) and entities (NGOs, Section 8 companies, Post Offices, NBFCs-ND).

#### 73. Written Agreements
⚠️ Obligation: Terms must be defined in written agreements and subject to legal vetting and Outsourcing Directions.

#### 74. Scope of activities
BCs may handle identification of borrowers, loan application processing, awareness creation, small value credit disbursal, and receipt/delivery of remittances.

#### 75. KYC Norms
⚠️ Obligation: Ensuring compliance with KYC and AML norms under the BC model remains the responsibility of the bank.

#### 78. Distance Criterion
Stipulates that the distance between a BC outlet and the base Banking Outlet should ordinarily not exceed 30 kms (rural/semi-urban) or 5 kms (metropolitan). This is removed for domestic scheduled commercial banks (Para 79).

#### 80. Payment of commission
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must pay reasonable commission to BCs. Agreements must prohibit BCs from charging any fee directly to customers.

#### C.10 Transactions put through BC
#### 82-83. Technology and Real-time accounting
⚠️ Obligation: Transactions must be accounted for on a real-time basis and integrated with the bank’s CBS.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are responsible for acts of omission/commission of the BC and for insuring the cash handled by BCs.

#### C.11 Internal Control & Monitoring
#### 84. Performance Review
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must monitor BC activities through Controlling Offices and review performance at least once a year.

#### C.13 Redressal of Grievances
#### 86. Grievance Machinery
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must constitute internal machinery for BC-related complaints. If a complaint is not resolved within 30 days, the customer can approach the RBI Ombudsman.

### Chapter VI – Doorstep Banking

#### 88. Eligibility
⚠️ Obligation: Banks require Board approval to offer doorstep banking.

#### 91. Modalities
⚠️ Obligation: If agents are used, banks must follow the RBI Directions on Managing Risks in Outsourcing.
⚠️ Obligation: Agents must be educated to detect forged and mutilated notes.

#### 92-96. Delivery Process
⚠️ Obligation: Cash delivery cannot be provided against telephonic requests.
⚠️ Obligation: Cash collected must be credited to the account on the same or next working day.

#### 99. Conditions
⚠️ Obligation: Doorstep services must only be offered to KYC-compliant customers.
⚠️ Obligation: The agreement must specify that the bank is responsible for the acts of the agent.

### Chapter VII – Information Reporting

#### 102. CISBI Portal
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must provide information on Banking Outlets, offices, and CSPs online on the Central Information System for Banking Infrastructure (CISBI) portal.

#### 105. Reporting Timelines
⚠️ Obligation: Information relating to opening, closure, merger, shifting, or conversion must be submitted no later than one week after the event.

#### 107. DBU Updates
⚠️ Obligation: DBU performance updates must be furnished to the Department of Supervision, RBI, on a monthly basis.

#### 114. NIL Report
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must submit a ‘NIL Report’ authentication by the 7th of every month confirming status in CISBI.

### Chapter VIII – Repeal and Other Provisions

#### 109. Repeal
These Directions supersede the circular dated November 28, 2025.

#### 111. Application of other laws
The provisions of these Directions are in addition to other laws in force.

## Definitions

*   **Administrative Office / Controlling Office:** A corporate/regional office exercising oversight; no direct interface with customers or banking transactions permitted.
*   **Back Office:** An office (like a CPC) attending to data processing and document verification; no direct interface with customers.
*   **Banking Outlet:** A fixed point service delivery unit manned by bank staff or BCs providing deposit, cash, or lending services for at least four hours a day, five days a week.
*   **Digital Banking Unit (DBU):** A specialized fixed point hub housing digital infrastructure for self-service or assisted digital banking.
*   **Unbanked Rural Centre (URC):** A rural (Tier 5 and 6) centre without a CBS-enabled Banking Outlet.
*   **Uniform Code Number (UCN):** A two-part code (Part-I for bank/branch and Part-II for district/population range) used for infrastructure reporting.
