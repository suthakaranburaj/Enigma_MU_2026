# Master Circular on SHG-Bank Linkage Programme

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/01 — FIDD.CO.FID.BC.No.4/12.01.033/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Priority Sector Lending
**Supersedes:** Consolidates all circulars issued on the subject up to March 31, 2025 (as listed in the Appendix)

---

## Summary

The SHG-Bank Linkage Programme is a key initiative designed to bridge the gap between the formal banking system and the rural poor, with a specific focus on financial inclusion and women's empowerment. This circular mandates that banks treat Self Help Group (SHG) financing as a core business activity, integrating it into all levels of credit planning. Banks are required to provide hassle-free credit for income-generating activities and social needs, utilize simplified KYC procedures for account opening, and ensure rigorous quarterly monitoring and reporting of the programme's progress to both the Board and NABARD.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
Primary (Urban) Cooperative Banks (UCBs) are expected to lay down, with the approval of their boards, transparent policies and guidelines for credit dispensation, in respect of each broad category of economic activity, keeping in view the credit exposure norms and various other guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India from time to time.
⚠️ Obligation: In order to ensure that the loan policy of the UCB reflects approved internal risk appetite and remains in alignment with the extant regulations, it shall be reviewed by the Board at least once in a financial year.

### 2. Working Capital Requirements
Assessment of working capital for SHG-linked activities should be flexible and include consumption expenditures.
⚠️ Obligation: The approach to financing of SHGs should be totally hassle-free.

### 4. Opening of Savings Bank A/C
SHGs, registered or unregistered, engaged in promoting savings habits are eligible to open accounts. They do not need prior credit history with the bank.
⚠️ Obligation: Instructions on simplified Customer Due Diligence (CDD) applicable to SHGs as prescribed in Chapter VI of the Master Direction - Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016 shall be adhered to.

### 5. Lending to SHGs
⚠️ Obligation: Bank lending to SHGs should be included in branch credit plan, block credit plan, district credit plan, and state credit plan of each bank. Utmost priority should be accorded to the sector. It should also form an integral part of the bank’s corporate credit plan.
⚠️ Obligation: As per operational guidelines issued by NABARD, SHGs may be sanctioned savings linked loans (varying from a saving to loan ratio of 1:1 to 1:4). In case of matured SHGs, loans may be given beyond the limit of four times the savings as per the discretion of the bank.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should strive to remove all operational irritants and make arrangements to expeditiously sanction and disburse credit by delegating adequate sanctioning powers to branch managers.

### 6. Interest Rates
⚠️ Obligation: Banks have discretion to decide interest rates on loans to SHGs/members, subject to regulatory guidelines on interest rate on advances contained in the Master Direction - Reserve Bank of India (Interest Rate on Advances) Directions, 2016.

### 7. Service/ Processing Charges
⚠️ Obligation: Instructions on service charges applicable to priority sector loans to SHGs as prescribed in the Master Directions - Reserve Bank of India (Priority Sector Lending – Targets and Classification) Directions, 2025 shall be adhered to.

### 8. Priority Sector Lending classification
Loans to SHGs are eligible for classification under Priority Sector Lending (PSL) under respective categories viz Agriculture, MSME, Social Infrastructure, and others.

### 9. Defaulters in SHGs
⚠️ Obligation: Defaults by a few members of SHGs and/or their family members should not ordinarily come in the way of financing SHGs per se, provided the SHG itself is not in default.
⚠️ Obligation: The bank loan may not be utilized by the SHG for financing a defaulter member to the bank.

### 10. Capacity Building and Training
⚠️ Obligation: Banks may initiate suitable steps to internalize the SHGs linkage project and organize exclusive short duration programmes for field level functionaries.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall adhere to the instructions on conducting tailored financial literacy programmes targeting SHGs.

### 11. Monitoring and Review of SHG Lending
⚠️ Obligation: Monitoring of SHG-BLP shall be a regular item on the agenda for discussion at the SLBC and DCC meetings.
⚠️ Obligation: It should be reviewed at the highest corporate level on a quarterly basis.
⚠️ Obligation: Progress under SHG-BLP shall be reported to NABARD (Micro Credit Innovations Department), Mumbai, on a quarterly basis, in the prescribed format within 15 days from due date.

### 12. Reporting to CICs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are advised to adhere to the guidelines on credit information reporting in respect of SHGs members contained in Master Direction – Reserve Bank of India (Credit Information Reporting) Directions, 2025.

## Definitions

- **Self Help Group (SHG):** Small groups (formal or informal) of individuals (normally 10-20 members) promoting savings habits among members, which are then lent to members for income generation.
- **Joint Liability Group (JLG):** An informal group of individuals (normally 4-10 members) coming together for the purpose of availing bank loans against mutual guarantee for similar economic activities.
- **Matured SHG:** SHGs that have shown a proven track record, savings pattern, and recovery rate, eligible for higher credit limits beyond the standard saving-to-loan ratio.
- **Simplified CDD:** Eased Know Your Customer requirements specifically tailored for SHGs as per Chapter VI of the KYC Master Direction.
