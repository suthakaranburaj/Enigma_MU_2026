# Master Circular - Guarantees, Co-acceptances & Letters of Credit - UCBs

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/11 (DoR.STR.REC 07/09.27.000/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks (UCBs)
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DoR.STR.REC.3/09.27.000/2024-25 dated April 01, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular provides a consolidated framework for Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks (UCBs) regarding the conduct of non-fund-based credit business, specifically Guarantees, Co-acceptances, and Letters of Credit (LCs). The circular outlines prudential limits on exposure, maturity restrictions (maximum 10 years), and mandatory safeguards to prevent the issuance of accommodation bills or unauthorized LCs. It emphasizes the absolute obligation of banks to honor commitments immediately upon invocation to maintain the credibility of the payment mechanism. It also sets specific margin requirements for commodities under Selective Credit Controls and mandates staff accountability for procedural lapses.

## Sections

### 1. Guarantees

#### 1.1 Issue of Guarantees

#### 1.1.1 Broad Guidelines
UCBs should extend guarantees within restricted limits so that their financial position is not impaired.

#### 1.1.2 Purpose
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: As a general rule, banks may provide only financial guarantees and not performance guarantees.
(ii) However, scheduled banks may issue performance guarantees on behalf of their constituents subject to exercising due caution.

#### 1.1.3 Maturity
⚠️ Obligation: It would be desirable for UCBs to confine their guarantees to relatively short-term maturities. Guarantees should not be issued for periods exceeding ten years in any case.

#### 1.1.4 Volume
⚠️ Obligation: The total volume of guarantee obligations outstanding at any time may not exceed 10 per cent of the total owned resources of the bank comprising paid up capital, reserves and deposits.
⚠️ Obligation: Within the overall ceiling, proportion of unsecured guarantees outstanding at any time may be limited to an amount equivalent to 25% of the owned funds (paid up capital + reserves) of the bank or 25% of the total amount of guarantees, whichever is less.

#### 1.1.5 Secured Guarantees
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should preferably issue secured guarantees. A secured guarantee means a guarantee made on the security of assets (including cash margin), the market value of which will not at any time be less than the amount of the contingent liability.

#### 1.1.6 Unsecured Guarantees
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should avoid undue concentration of unsecured guarantee commitments. The Board of Directors should fix suitable proportions for individual constituents so they do not exceed a reasonable proportion of total unsecured obligations or a reasonable multiple of shareholdings.

#### 1.1.7 Deferred Payment Guarantees
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should generally provide deferred payment guarantees backed by adequate tangible securities or counter-guarantees of the Central/State Government, FIs, or other banks.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure total credit facilities, including proposed deferred payment guarantees, do not exceed prescribed exposure ceilings.

#### 1.2 Guarantees in respect of Commodities covered under Selective Credit Controls
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs should not issue guarantees for importers of essential commodities without taking a cash margin equivalent to at least one half (50%) of the amount payable under the guarantee.

#### 1.3 Safeguards in Issuance of Guarantees
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall appraise proposals for guarantees with the same diligence as fund-based limits.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Bank guarantees should be issued in security forms serially numbered.
(iii) ⚠️ Obligation: Guarantees above a particular cut-off point must be issued under two signatures in triplicate.
(iv) ⚠️ Obligation: Guarantees should not normally be allowed to customers who do not enjoy credit facilities but only maintain current accounts. Scrutiny of financial status and Wealth-tax/Income-tax returns is required.

#### 1.4 Payment under Bank Guarantees - Immediate Settlement of Cases
(iv) ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs should honor bank guarantees issued by them promptly on their invocation. Reluctance to honor commitments brings the banking system into disrepute.

#### 1.5 Delay in Obtaining Certified Copies of Judgments
(ii) procedure:
(a) ⚠️ Obligation: Where the bank is a party to a proceeding decided in favor of the Government, the bank should not insist on production of a certified copy if the order is pronounced in open court and available on court websites.
(b) ⚠️ Obligation: If the bank is not a party, an attested copy of the judgment from the Government Counsel is sufficient.

#### 1.6 Correspondence with Government Departments
⚠️ Obligation: Correspondence must be exchanged with the concerned Government Ministry/Departments, not the President of India.

### 2. Co-acceptance of Bills

#### 2.1 Irregularities in Co-acceptance of Bills
Banks have often co-accepted accommodation bills where no genuine trade transaction took place, leading to difficulty in recovery.

#### 2.2 Safeguards
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: Co-acceptance limits should be extended only to customers enjoying other limits with the bank.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Only genuine trade bills should be co-accepted; banks must ensure goods are actually received in the stock accounts of borrowers.
(v) ⚠️ Obligation: The powers to co-accept bills beyond a stipulated limit must be exercised by two authorized officials jointly.
(xiii) ⚠️ Obligation: Co-acceptances for ₹10,000/- and above should be signed by two officials jointly.
(xiv) ⚠️ Obligation: Before discounting bills co-accepted by other banks for ₹2 lakh and above from a single party, the bank should obtain written confirmation from the accepting bank's controlling office.
(xv) ⚠️ Obligation: When the value of total bills discounted (co-accepted by other banks) exceeds ₹20 lakh for a single borrower/group, prior written approval of the Head Office of the co-accepting bank must be obtained.

### 3. Letters of Credit (LCs)

#### 3.1 Guidelines for Grant of LCs Facility
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs should not normally grant LC facilities to parties who maintain only nominal current accounts. Proper enquiries into antecedents and financial position are mandatory.

#### 3.2 LCs for Commodities covered under Selective Credit Controls
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are not permitted to open inland LCs providing a clause therein which would enable other banks to discount usance bills under the LCs.

#### 3.3 Safeguards in Opening of LCs
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: LCs must be issued in security forms only.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Large LCs must be issued under two authorized signatures, including one from HO/Controlling Office.
(viii) ⚠️ Obligation: In no case should working capital limits be utilized for retiring bills pertaining to the acquisition of capital assets.
(ix) ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should not extend non-fund based facilities or ad-hoc facilities to parties who are not their regular constituents.

#### 3.4 Payment under LCs - Immediate Settlement of Claims
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should honor their commitments under LCs and make payments promptly. Suitable action should be taken against officials/constituents in case of fraudulent issues or criminal conspiracy.

### 4. Other Common Guidelines

#### 4.1 Credit Exposure Norms and Statutory / Other Restrictions
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs are required to strictly observe exposure norms and statutory restrictions for non-fund based limits.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Ceilings for total credit exposure (including non-fund), unsecured guarantees, and advances to Directors/relatives must be strictly observed.

#### 4.4 Management of "Funded" Limits
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must lay down clear instructions for staff when non-funded facilities become "funded" due to devolvement or invocation. Goods covered under devolved bills must remain under bank control/hypothecation.

#### 4.5 Parking of Dues
⚠️ Obligation: Debts arising from devolved LCs or invoked guarantees must be treated as part of the borrower's principal operating account for NPA identification purposes. Parking dues in separate accounts to avoid NPA classification is prohibited.

#### 4.6 Information Sharing
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must obtain declarations from borrowers about credit facilities enjoyed from other banks and exchange information at least at quarterly intervals.
⚠️ Obligation: For borrowers with limits of ₹5.00 crore and above, banks should introduce a formal system of exchange of information.

#### 4.8 Non-resident Guarantees
⚠️ Obligation: Category-I AD Banks must ensure compliance with A.P. (DIR Series) Circular No. 18 dated October 04, 2024 regarding due diligence for non-resident guarantees.

## Definitions

- **Owned Funds:** Comprises paid-up capital and reserves of the bank.
- **Owned Resources:** Comprises paid-up capital, reserves, and deposits of the bank.
- **Secured Guarantee:** A guarantee made on the security of assets (including cash margin) where market value is not less than the contingent liability, or those covered by Central/State Govt/FI counter-guarantees.
- **Unsecured Exposure:** An exposure where the realisable value of security is not more than 10% of the outstanding exposure.
- **Essential Commodities:** Commodities specified by the RBI from time to time under Selective Credit Controls.
