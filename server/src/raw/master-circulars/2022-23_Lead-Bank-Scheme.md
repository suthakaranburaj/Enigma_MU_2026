# Master Circular – Lead Bank Scheme

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/04, FIDD.CO.LBS.BC.No.03/02.01.001/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks (including Small Finance Banks), SLBC/UTLBC Convenor Banks, Lead Banks.
**Regulatory Topic:** Priority Sector Lending
**Supersedes:** None (Consolidates all relevant guidelines issued up to March 31, 2025).

---

## Summary

The Lead Bank Scheme (LBS) is a landmark initiative designed to coordinate the activities of banks and other developmental agencies to enhance the flow of bank finance to priority sectors and promote rural development. Administered through a tiered structure of committees—Block Level Bankers’ Committee (BLBC), District Consultative Committee (DCC), District Level Review Committee (DLRC), and State Level Bankers’ Committee (SLBC)—the scheme ensures decentralized credit planning via Potential Linked Credit Plans (PLPs) and Annual Credit Plans (ACPs). Key focus areas include achieving 100% financial inclusion, maintaining healthy Credit-Deposit (CD) ratios, facilitating Direct Benefit Transfers (DBT), and deepening the digital payments ecosystem.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
(i) The Lead Bank Scheme (LBS) was introduced in December 1969 based on the 'Area Approach' recommended by the Gadgil Study Group and Nariman Committee.
(iii) ⚠️ Obligation: Pursuent to recommendations, a particular bank is assigned ‘Lead Bank’ responsibility of a district to assume a leadership role for coordinating efforts of credit institutions and the Government.
(vi) ⚠️ Obligation: Lead Banks were advised to ensure that Private Sector Banks (PvSBs) are more closely and actively involved in the implementation of the LBS and preparation of the District Credit Plan (DCP).

### 2. Fora under Lead Bank Scheme

#### 2.1 Block Level Bankers’ Committee (BLBC)
⚠️ Obligation: BLBC meetings are held at quarterly intervals. All branch managers must attend and enrich discussions.

#### 2.2 District Consultative Committee (DCC)
2.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The District Collector is the Chairman. DCC facilitates coordination in implementing developmental activities.
2.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: DCC meetings should be convened by the Lead Banks at quarterly intervals.

#### 2.2.4 Role of Lead District Managers (LDMs)
⚠️ Obligation: The office of LDM should be sufficiently strengthened with infrastructural support (office space, technical infrastructure, and a dedicated vehicle).
⚠️ Obligation: New functions for LDMs include monitoring DCP implementation, associating with FLCs/RSETIs, and arranging quarterly grievance redressal public meetings.

#### 2.2.6 District Level Review Committee (DLRC) Meetings
⚠️ Obligation: DLRC meetings should be convened by the Lead Banks at least once in a quarter. Public representatives (MPs/MLAs/Zilla Parishad Chiefs) shall invariably be invited.

#### 2.2.7 DCC/DLRC meetings – Annual Calendar of Meetings
⚠️ Obligation: Lead Banks must prepare an Annual Schedule of DCC and DLRC meetings on a calendar year basis and circulate it at the beginning of each year. DCC and DLRC meetings must not be held simultaneously.

#### 2.3 State Level Bankers’ Committee (SLBC)
2.3.1 ⚠️ Obligation: SLBC is an apex inter-institutional forum chaired by the Chairman/MD of the Convenor Bank. Meetings are held on a quarterly basis.
2.3.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Meetings are co-chaired by the Additional Chief Secretary or Development Commissioner of the State.
2.3.4 ⚠️ Obligation: SLBC Convenor Banks must prepare a yearly calendar of programmes by 15th January every year. Meeting minutes must be forwarded to stakeholders within 10 days of the meeting.

### 3. Implementation of Lead Bank Scheme

#### 3.2 Potential Linked Credit Plans (PLPs)
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: NABARD to ensure PLPs are focused and implementable. PLPs must emphasize sustainable agricultural practices (reducing carbon footprint, water efficiency, etc.).
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: A pre-PLP meeting must be convened by the LDM during June every year.

#### 3.3 Monitoring the Performance of Credit Plans
⚠️ Obligation: Data on ACP is reviewed at Block (BLBC), District (DCC/DLRC), and State (SLBC) levels. Quarterly statements on progress under ACP must be prepared in the single format prescribed in Annex IV.

#### 3.4 Revised mechanism of Data Flow for LBS fora meetings
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must develop a standardized system to download LBS data directly from CBS/MIS to Excel to obviate manual data entry.
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: Head Offices must generate the LBS report by the 15th of the month following the quarter-end.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Controlling Offices must upload this data to the SLBC portal by the 20th of the month following the quarter-end.

### 5. Banking Penetration
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Lead Banks/SLBCs must focus on achieving 100% financial inclusion. Banks should consider opening CBS-enabled banking outlets in unbanked rural centres (URCs).
5.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must open at least 25% of total banking outlets in URCs (Tier 5 and 6 centres).

### 6. Credit Deposit Ratio (CD Ratio)
6.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are advised to achieve a CD Ratio of 60% in rural and semi-urban branches separately on an All-India basis.
6.2 ⚠️ Obligation: In districts with a CD Ratio < 40%, Special Sub-Committees (SSCs) of the DCC must be set up to draw up Monitorable Action Plans (MAPs).
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: SSC plans must be monitored once every two months.

### 7. Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must complete Aadhaar seeding in all bank accounts and closely monitor progress. A system must be in place to provide beneficiaries with seeding acknowledgments and confirmations.

### 8. Service Area Approach (SAA)
(ii) SAA restrictive provisions were dispensed with in 2004, except for Government Sponsored Schemes. Borrowers have the choice of approaching any branch.

#### 8.1 Dispensing with ‘No Due Certificate’
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must dispense with obtaining a ‘No Due Certificate’ from individual borrowers (including SHGs/JLGs) in rural and semi-urban areas for all types of loans. This applies to urban areas and metropolitan cities as well.

### 10. Expanding and Deepening of Digital Payments Ecosystem
⚠️ Obligation: SLBCs/UTLBCs must identify one district on a pilot basis to make it 100% digitally enabled, then devise a roadmap to cover all districts.

## Definitions

- **Lead Bank:** A bank designated by the RBI to coordinate banking and credit activities in a specific district.
- **Lead District Manager (LDM):** The officer designated by the Lead Bank to manage LBS activities at the district level.
- **Service Area Approach (SAA):** A scheme where a specific area is assigned to a bank branch to increase productive lending.
- **Panchayat Samiti:** A rural local government body at the intermediate (block) level.
- **Unbanked Rural Centre (URC):** A rural centre (Tier 5 or 6) that does not have a CBS-enabled banking outlet.
- **Credit-Deposit (CD) Ratio:** The ratio of how much a bank lends out of the deposits it has mobilized.
