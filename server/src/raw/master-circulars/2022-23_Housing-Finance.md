## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the regulatory framework for housing finance provided by Scheduled Commercial Banks. It outlines permissible lending activities, including land acquisition (subject to construction declarations), construction of dwelling units, and slum improvement schemes. It mandates strict adherence to Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratios and risk weights to prevent real estate speculation. Furthermore, it incorporates critical judicial directives from the Delhi and Bombay High Courts regarding unauthorized construction and disclosure requirements for builders, ensuring consumer protection and credit discipline.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
Banks occupy a strategic position in the financial system and play an important role in providing credit to the housing sector.

### 2. Various Regulations
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must ensure that bank credit is used for production and construction activities and not for activities connected with speculation in real estate.

#### (A) ACQUISITION OF LAND
⚠️ Obligation: Bank finance can be granted only for purchase of a plot, provided a declaration is obtained from the borrower that he intends to construct a house on the said plot within such period as may be laid down by the banks.

#### (B) CONSTRUCTION OF BUILDING / READY-BUILT HOUSE
(i) Banks may grant loans for purchase/construction of dwelling units and repairs.
(ii) Finance is permitted for a second house for self-occupation.
(vii) ⚠️ Obligation: In light of Delhi High Court observations on unauthorized construction:
(a) Banks must obtain a copy of the sanctioned plan before sanctioning the home loan.
(b) An affidavit-cum-undertaking must be obtained stating construction shall not violate the sanctioned plan.
(c) An Architect must certify construction stages and final completion.
(f) No loan should be given for properties in unauthorized colonies until regularized.
(g) No loan for residential properties intended for commercial use.
