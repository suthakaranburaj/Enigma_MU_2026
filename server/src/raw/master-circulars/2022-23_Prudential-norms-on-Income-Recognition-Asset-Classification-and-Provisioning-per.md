# Master Circular - Prudential norms on Income Recognition, Asset Classification and Provisioning pertaining to Advances

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/13 — DOR.STR.REC.9/21.04.048/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified (Note: Marked as "Withdrawn" in source)
**Applicable To:** All Commercial Banks (excluding RRBs)
**Regulatory Topic:** Asset Classification
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.STR.REC.8/21.04.048/2024-25 dated April 2, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the prudential norms for Income Recognition, Asset Classification, and Provisioning (IRAC) applicable to the advances portfolio of commercial banks. It establishes objective criteria for identifying Non-Performing Assets (NPAs) and Special Mention Accounts (SMAs), ensuring that income is recognized only on a realization basis for distressed loans. The circular outlines a phased framework for the resolution of stressed assets, specifies provisioning requirements for different asset categories (Substandard, Doubtful, and Loss), and mandates robust board oversight and data integrity for credit risk management.

## Sections

### Part A

### 1. GENERAL
1.1-1.3 Introduces prudential norms to move towards consistency and transparency in published accounts based on recommendations by the Committee on the Financial System (Shri M. Narasimham).

### 2. DEFINITIONS
#### 2.1 Non-performing Assets
2.1.1-2.1.2 An asset becomes non-performing when it ceases to generate income for the bank.
⚠️ Obligation: A loan is an NPA where:
i. Interest/principal remains overdue for >90 days (Term Loans).
ii. Account remains 'out of order' (CC/OD).
iii. Bill remains overdue for >90 days (Bills Discounted).
iv. Overdue for two crop seasons (Short duration crops) or one crop season (Long duration crops).
v. Liquidity facility in securitisation remains outstanding for >90 days.
vi. Derivative receivables remain unpaid for 90 days from the due date.

#### 2.2 ‘Out of Order’ status
⚠️ Obligation: A CC/OD account is 'out of order' if the balance is continuously in excess of the limit for 90 days, or there are no credits for 90 days, or credits are insufficient to cover interest debited in the previous 90-day period.

#### 2.3 ‘Overdue’ status
⚠️ Obligation: Any amount is overdue if not paid on the due date. Accounts must be flagged as overdue as part of day-end processes on the due date.

### 3. INCOME RECOGNITION
#### 3.1 Income Recognition Policy
⚠️ Obligation: Policy must be objective and based on record of recovery. Banks shall not charge and take to income account interest on any NPA, including Govt guaranteed accounts.

#### 3.2 Reversal of income
⚠️ Obligation: If an account becomes NPA, entire interest accrued and credited to income in past periods must be reversed if unrealized.

#### 3.4 Interest Application
⚠️ Obligation: Reverse interest already charged but uncollected by debiting P&L and stop further application of interest on NPAs. Accrued interest may be recorded in a Memorandum account.

### 4. ASSET CLASSIFICATION
#### 4.1 Categories of NPAs
⚠️ Obligation: Classify NPAs into:
i. Substandard: Remained NPA for ≤ 12 months.
ii. Doubtful: Remained in substandard category for 12 months.
iii. Loss: Identified as uncollectible by bank/auditors/RBI but not yet written off.

#### 4.2 Guidelines for classification of assets
4.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must establish automated systems for identification of NPAs.
4.2.5 ⚠️ Obligation: Upgrade NPA to 'standard' only if entire arrears of interest and principal are paid by the borrower.
4.2.7 ⚠️ Obligation: Asset classification must be borrower-wise, not facility-wise. If one facility is NPA, all facilities to that borrower are NPA.
4.2.9 ⚠️ Obligation: Accounts with significant erosion in security (>50% value) should be straightaway classified as Doubtful. If security is <10% of outstanding, classify as Loss asset.

### 5. PROVISIONING NORMS
#### 5.1 General
Lenders and statutory auditors are responsible for making adequate provisions.

#### 5.2-5.4 Specific Provisioning Requirements
⚠️ Obligation:
- **Loss Assets:** 100% provision.
- **Doubtful Assets (Unsecured):** 100% provision.
- **Doubtful Assets (Secured):** 25% (up to 1 year), 40% (1-3 years), 100% (>3 years).
- **Substandard Assets:** 15% general provision; 25% if unsecured.

#### 5.5 Standard assets
⚠️ Obligation: General provisions:
- Farm credit/SME/Residential Housing: 0.25%.
- Commercial Real Estate (CRE): 1.00%.
- CRE-Residential Housing: 0.75%.
- All other loans: 0.40%.

#### 5.10 Provisioning Coverage Ratio (PCR)
⚠️ Obligation: Maintain a PCR (ratio of provisioning to gross NPAs) of not less than 70%.

### 6. Writing-off of NPAs
6.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must extinguish all recovery means before writing off. Full details of partial and technical write-offs must be disclosed in 'Notes to Accounts'.

### PART B1 - Framework for Resolution of Stressed Assets
#### 8. Early identification and reporting of stress
⚠️ Obligation: Classify accounts into SMA categories immediately on default:
- SMA-0: 1-30 days overdue.
- SMA-1: 31-60 days overdue.
- SMA-2: 61-90 days overdue.

#### 9. Implementation of Resolution Plan (RP)
⚠️ Obligation: All lenders must initiate a prima facie review of a borrower within 30 days of default (Review Period) to decide on a resolution strategy.
⚠️ Obligation: Lenders must enter into an Inter-Creditor Agreement (ICA). Decisions agreed by 75% by value and 60% by number are binding.

#### 10. Implementation Conditions for RP
⚠️ Obligation: RPs for aggregate exposure ≥ ₹100 crore require an Independent Credit Evaluation (ICE) by an authorized Credit Rating Agency (CRA).

#### 11. Delayed Implementation of Resolution Plan
⚠️ Obligation: If a viable RP is not implemented within 180 days from the end of the review period, make an additional provision of 20%. If delayed to 365 days, total additional provision must reach 35%.

### PART B2: Prudential Norms Applicable to Restructuring
⚠️ Obligation: Restructured accounts classified as 'standard' must be immediately downgraded to 'substandard'. Upgradation is only possible after a 'specified period' of satisfactory performance (usually 1 year after the first payment).

### PART C – Miscellaneous
#### 28. Wilful Defaulters
⚠️ Obligation: Accounts of companies with directors on the wilful defaulter list attract accelerated provisioning (e.g., 25% to 100% depending on the NPA age and security).

## Definitions

- **Non-performing Asset (NPA):** A loan or advance where interest/principal instalment remains overdue for more than 90 days.
- **Out of Order:** Status for CC/OD accounts where the balance remains continuously above the limit or there are insufficient credits to cover interest.
- **Overdue:** Any amount due to the bank not paid on the fixed due date.
- **Restructuring:** An act where a lender, for economic or legal reasons relating to the borrower's financial difficulty, grants concessions.
- **Specified Period:** One year from the commencement of the first payment of interest or principal under a restructuring package.
- **Technical Write-off:** Writing off NPAs at the Head Office level without foregoing the right to recovery at the branch level.
