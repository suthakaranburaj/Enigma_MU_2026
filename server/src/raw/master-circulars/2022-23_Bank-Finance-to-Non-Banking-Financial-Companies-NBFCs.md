
# Master Circular - Bank Finance to Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/15 (DOR.CRE.REC.No.05/21.04.172/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks (excluding Regional Rural Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.CRE.REC.No.17/21.04.172/2024-25 dated April 24, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates all existing instructions issued by the Reserve Bank of India up to March 31, 2025, regarding bank financing to Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs). It outlines the regulatory policy governing credit dispensation to registered and exempted NBFCs, while maintaining specific restrictions on sensitive activities such as financing for IPOs, bridge loans, and secondary market share purchases. The circular establishes prudential exposure ceilings under the Large Exposures Framework and details the eligibility criteria for financing factoring companies. It serves as a statutory guideline under Section 35A of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
Reserve Bank of India has been regulating the financial activities of the Non-Banking Financial Companies under the provisions of Chapter III B of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934. In terms of Section 45 IA of the said Act and Section 29 A of the National Housing Bank Act, 1987, all NBFCs including Housing Finance Companies have to be mandatorily registered with the RBI.

#### 1.1 Terminology
a. 'Current investments' means the investments classified in the balance sheet of the borrower as 'current assets' and are intended to be held for less than one year.
b. 'Long term investments' means all types of investments other than that classified as 'current assets'.
c. 'NBFCs' means the Non-Banking Financial Companies registered with the Reserve Bank of India, which shall also include Housing Finance Company (HFC) registered under Section 29A of the National Housing Bank Act, 1987.
d. 'Unsecured loans' means the loans not secured by any tangible asset.

#### 1.2 Background
The credit related matters of banks have been progressively deregulated. However, in view of the sensitivities attached to financing of certain types of activities undertaken by NBFCs, restrictions on financing of such activities continue to be in force.

### 2. Bank Finance to NBFCs registered with RBI
2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The ceiling on bank credit linked to Net Owned Fund (NOF) of NBFCs has been withdrawn for all NBFCs statutorily registered with RBI and engaged in principal business of asset financing, loan, factoring and investment activities. Banks may extend need based working capital facilities and term loans.
2.2 Banks may also extend finance to NBFCs against second hand assets financed by them.
2.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks may formulate suitable loan policy with the approval of their Boards of Directors within the prudential guidelines and exposure norms prescribed by the Reserve Bank to extend various kinds of credit facilities to NBFCs subject to the condition that prohibited activities are not financed.

### 3. Bank Finance to NBFCs not requiring Registration
⚠️ Obligation: For NBFCs exempted from registration (as per Master Direction - Exemptions from the provisions of RBI Act, 1934), banks may take credit decisions on the basis of usual factors like purpose of credit, nature and quality of underlying assets, repayment capacity, and risk perception.

### 4. Activities not eligible for Bank Credit
4.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The following activities undertaken by NBFCs are not eligible for bank credit:
(i) Bills discounted / rediscounted by NBFCs, except for rediscounting of bills arising from sale of commercial vehicles and two/three wheeler vehicles, subject to specific conditions (bills drawn by manufacturer on dealers only; representing genuine sale; verification of bona fides).
(ii) Investments of NBFCs in shares, debentures, etc. (Stock Broking Companies may receive need-based credit against stock-in-trade).
(iii) Unsecured loans / inter-corporate deposits by NBFCs to / in any company.
(iv) All types of loans and advances by NBFCs to their subsidiaries, group companies / entities.
(v) Finance to NBFCs for further lending to individuals for subscribing to IPOs and for purchase of shares from secondary market.

#### 4.2 Leased and Sub-Leased Assets
⚠️ Obligation: As banks can extend financial assistance to equipment leasing companies, they should not enter into lease agreements departmentally with such companies as well as other NBFCs engaged in equipment leasing.

### 5. Bank Finance to Factoring Companies
5.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks can extend financial assistance to NBFC-Factors and NBFC-ICCs holding certificate of registration under the Factoring Regulation Act, 2011.
5.2 ⚠️ Obligation: To be eligible, NBFC-Factors must satisfy:
(i) Derive at least 50 per cent of their income from factoring activity.
(ii) Receivables purchased/financed form at least 50 per cent of their assets.
(iii) Assets/income relating to bill discounting facilities must be excluded from these calculations.

### 6. Other Prohibitions on Bank Finance to NBFCs
#### 6.1 Bridge loans / interim finance
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should not grant bridge loans of any nature, or interim finance against capital / debenture issues and / or in the form of loans of a bridging nature pending raising of long-term funds from the market to all categories of NBFCs.

#### 6.2 Advances against collateral security of shares to NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Shares and debentures cannot be accepted as collateral securities for secured loans granted to NBFC borrowers for any purpose.

#### 6.3 Restriction on guarantees for placement of funds with NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall comply with instructions under paragraph 2.4 of Master Circular - Guarantees and Co-acceptances dated April 01, 2025 regarding restrictions on issuing guarantees for placement of funds with NBFCs.

### 7. Prudential Ceilings for Exposure of Banks to NBFCs
7.1 Exposure computation as per Circular on Large Exposures Framework dated June 03, 2019.
7.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Exposure to a single NBFC (excl. gold loan companies) restricted to 20% of eligible Tier I capital base. Exposure to a group of connected NBFCs restricted to 25% of Tier I Capital.
7.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Exposure to a single NBFC predominantly engaged in gold jewellery lending (50% or more financial assets) shall not exceed 7.5% of the bank's capital funds (Tier I + Tier II). Limit can increase to 12.5% if additional exposure is for on-lending to infrastructure.
7.4 Banks may consider fixing internal limits for aggregate exposure to all NBFCs.
7.5 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should have an internal sub-limit on aggregate exposures to all NBFCs having gold loans to the extent of 50% or more of total financial assets.
7.6 Capital infusion after the published balance sheet date may be considered for ceilings subject to external auditor's certificate and submission to RBI (Department of Supervision).
7.7 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall adhere to intra-group limits in accordance with Guidelines on Management of Intra-Group Transactions and Exposures dated February 11, 2014.

### 8. Restrictions Regarding Investments Made by Banks in Securities / Instruments Issued by NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should follow guidelines provided under paragraph 30 of chapter IX “Investment in Non-SLR Securities” of Master Direction - Classification, Valuation and Operation of Investment Portfolio of Commercial Banks (Directions), 2023.

### 9. Risk Weights for Bank Credit to NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks’ exposure to NBFCs shall be subject to capital charge as per Risk Weight prescribed in paragraph 5 of Master Circular – Basel III Capital Regulations dated April 01, 2025.

## Definitions

- **NBFCs:** Non-Banking Financial Companies registered with the RBI, including Housing Finance Companies (HFCs).
- **Current investments:** Investments intended to be held for less than one year.
- **Long term investments:** Investments other than current investments.
- **Unsecured loans:** Loans not secured by any tangible asset.
- **NBFC-Factors:** NBFCs holding a certificate of registration under the Factoring Regulation Act, 2011 and meeting the 50% income/asset criteria.
