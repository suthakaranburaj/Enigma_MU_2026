# Master Circular - Prudential Norms on Capital Adequacy - Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks (UCBs)

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/09 — DOR.CAP.REC.03/09.18.201/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Capital Adequacy
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.CAP.REC.5/09.18.201/2024-25 dated April 01, 2024

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the prudential norms regarding capital adequacy for Urban Co-operative Banks (UCBs). It establishes a tiered regulatory framework (Tiers 1 through 4) based on deposit size and geographical reach. UCBs are mandated to maintain minimum Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets Ratios (CRAR) of 9% or 12% depending on their tier, alongside specific minimum net worth requirements (₹2 crore or ₹5 crore). The circular details the components of Tier I and Tier II capital, provides a comprehensive list of risk weights for various asset classes, and sets guidelines for the issuance of regulatory capital instruments like preference shares and long-term subordinated bonds.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
Capital acts as a buffer in times of crisis. Adequacy of capital is a pre-condition for licensing and continuance of business.

### 2. Statutory Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: In terms of Section 11 of BR Act (AACS), no co-operative bank shall carry on business unless the aggregate value of paid-up capital and reserves is at least one lakh rupees.

### 3. Net Worth
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall have minimum net worth as under:
*   Tier 1 UCBs operating in a single district: ₹2 crore.
*   All other UCBs (all tiers): ₹5 crore.
*   Phased achievement: At least 50% of the requirement by March 31, 2026, and 100% by March 31, 2028.

### 4. Capital Adequacy Norms
⚠️ Obligation: Tier 1 UCBs must maintain a minimum CRAR of 9% of Risk Weighted Assets (RWAs).
⚠️ Obligation: Tier 2 to 4 UCBs must maintain a minimum CRAR of 12% of RWAs.
⚠️ Obligation: Tier 2 to 4 UCBs not meeting 12% must reach 10% by March 31, 2024; 11% by March 31, 2025; and 12% by March 31, 2026.

#### 4.1 Tier I Capital
Tier I comprises:
(i) Paid-up share capital from regular members.
(ii) Contributions from associate/nominal members (subject to restrictions).
(iii) Non-refundable admission fees.
(iv) Perpetual Non-Cumulative Preference Shares (PNCPS).
(v) Free Reserves (excluding reserves for anticipated losses/frauds).
(vi) Capital Reserves (surplus from asset sales).
(vii) Perpetual Debt Instruments (PDIs).
(viii) Net surplus in P&L account.
(ix) Special Reserve under Section 36(1)(viii) of Income Tax Act.
(x) Revaluation reserves (at 55% discount, subject to specific audit and valuation conditions).

#### 4.2 Tier II Capital
Tier II comprises:
4.2.1 General Provisions and Loss Reserves (admitted up to 1.25% of total RWAs).
4.2.2 Investment Fluctuation Reserve.
4.2.3 Tier II capital instruments:
a) Upper Tier II: Perpetual Cumulative Preference Shares (PCPS), Redeemable Non-Cumulative Preference Shares (RNCPS), Redeemable Cumulative Preference Shares (RCPS).
b) Lower Tier II: Long Term Subordinated Bonds (LTSB).

### 5. Capital for Market Risk
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must assign an additional risk weight of 2.5% on investments for market risk.
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must assign a risk weight of 100% on open position limits for foreign exchange and gold.

### 6. Share linking to Borrowings
⚠️ Obligation: Borrowings (except against FDs) must be linked to shareholdings:
(i) 5% of borrowings for unsecured basis.
(ii) 2.5% of borrowings for secured basis.
(iii) For MSEs (secured): 1% initially, 1.5% over the next 2 years.
(iv) Share-linking is discretionary for UCBs meeting minimum CRAR and having Tier 1 CRAR of 5.5%.

### 7. Refund of share capital
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs may refund share capital to members on demand only if the bank complies with minimum regulatory CRAR and the refund does not result in the bank falling below the minimum CRAR.

### 8. Measures for protection of investors in regulatory capital instruments
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must not use Fixed Deposit rates as a benchmark for floating rate instruments.
⚠️ Obligation: Application forms must include a specific acknowledgment sign-off by the investor.
⚠️ Obligation: Publicity materials must state in bold (Arial 14) that the instruments are different from fixed deposits and not covered by deposit insurance.

### 9. Returns
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must furnish an annual return to Regional Offices indicating capital funds, off-balance sheet conversions, and CRAR calculations as per Annex 5.

## Definitions

- **Tier 1 UCBs:** All unit UCBs and salary earners’ UCBs (irrespective of size), and other UCBs with deposits up to ₹100 crore.
- **Tier 2 UCBs:** Deposits > ₹100 crore and up to ₹1,000 crore.
- **Tier 3 UCBs:** Deposits > ₹1,000 crore and up to ₹10,000 crore.
- **Tier 4 UCBs:** Deposits > ₹10,000 crore.
- **Net Worth:** Paid-up capital plus free reserves and other eligible items minus intangible assets and losses (detailed in Annex 1).
- **CRAR:** Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets Ratio, computed as Eligible Total Capital divided by Total Risk-Weighted Assets.
- **Free Reserves:** Reserves created out of net profit and which are not specific reserves.
- **Unsecured Exposure:** Exposure where the realisable value of security is not more than 10% ab-initio.
