# Master Circular - Housing Finance for UCBs

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/17 (DOR.CRE.REC.No.11/07.10.002/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.CRE.REC.No.6/07.10.002/2024-25 dated April 2, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular provides a consolidated framework for Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks (UCBs) to provide housing finance. It outlines the eligibility criteria for various borrowers (individuals, housing boards, and societies), sets maximum loan limits based on the bank's Tier classification, and defines margin requirements. The circular also prescribes strict aggregate exposure limits for the real estate sector and mandates specific safeguards against fraud and unauthorized constructions, including adherence to the National Building Code and judicial directions from the Delhi and Bombay High Courts.

## Sections

### 1. General
1.1 UCBs have a strategic role in provide credit to the housing sector, including priority sector lending.
1.2 Banks are permitted to grant housing loans from their own resources subject to specified guidelines.

### 2. Eligible Category of Borrowers
UCBs may grant loans to:
(a) Individuals and co-operative / group housing societies.
(b) Housing boards for EWS, LIG, and MIG schemes.
(c) Owners for extension and up-gradation.

### 3. Eligibility for Housing Finance
Eligible purposes include construction/purchase of flats, repairs/alterations, schemes for SC/ST, slum clearance, and educational/health centers part of housing projects.

### 4. Terms and Conditions for Housing Loans

#### 4.1 Maximum Loan Amount & Margins
⚠️ Obligation: (i) Boards must identify eligible borrowers and decide margins based on repayment capacity.
⚠️ Obligation: (ii) Individual loan ceilings:
- Tier 1: ₹60 lakh
- Tier 2: ₹1.40 crore
- Tier 3: ₹2 crore
- Tier 4: ₹3 crore
⚠️ Obligation: (iii) Single borrower exposure limited to 15% of Tier-I capital; Group exposure limited to 25% of Tier-I capital.

#### 4.2 Interest rate, foreclosure charges / prepayment penalty
⚠️ Obligation: 4.2.2 Banks are not permitted to charge foreclosure charges / prepayment penalties on floating rate home loans.
⚠️ Obligation: 4.2.3 Banks must put in place a policy framework for resetting floating interest rates on EMI-based loans, ensuring borrowers have options for elongation of tenor or increase in EMI.

#### 4.3 Penal Charges
⚠️ Obligation: 4.3.1 Penalty for non-compliance must be treated as ‘penal charges’ and not ‘penal interest’ added to the interest rate.

#### 4.4 Key Facts Statement (KFS)
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must comply with KFS instructions for loans and advances dated April 15, 2024.

#### 4.5 Security
4.5.1 Loans secured by mortgage of property, government guarantee, or both.
4.5.2 Alternative securities: LIC policies, Govt Promissory Notes, shares, gold ornaments.
⚠️ Obligation: 4.5.3 UCBs must comply with the September 13, 2023 instructions for the timely release of property documents upon loan repayment.

#### 4.6 Period of Loan
⚠️ Obligation: (i) Maximum repayment period is 20 years, including moratorium.

#### 4.8 Aggregate Housing/Real estate Limits
⚠️ Obligation: 4.8.1 Aggregate exposure to residential mortgages (excluding priority sector) shall not exceed 25 per cent of total loans and advances.
⚠️ Obligation: 4.8.2 Aggregate exposure to the real estate sector (excluding individual housing loans) shall not exceed five per cent of total loans and advances.
⚠️ Obligation: 4.8.6 UCBs shall not exceed prescribed limits even for funds obtained from higher financing agencies/NHB.

### 5. Additional / Supplementary Finance
5.1 UCBs may extend finance for repairs/alterations.
5.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Limit of ₹10 lakh in metropolitan centers and ₹6 lakh in other centers for repairs/additions, requiring certificates from qualified engineers/architects.

### 6. Lending to Housing Boards
6.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Loans to housing boards must involve a stipulation that the board ensures prompt recovery of installments from beneficiaries.

### 7. Advances to Builders / Contractors
7.1 Banks should normally refrain from sanctioning loans to this category to avoid dual financing.
7.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Financial assistance to contractors undertaking small construction without advance payments is allowed against hypothecation of materials.
7.4 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall not extend facilities to builders / contractors for land acquisition. Land accepted as collateral must be valued at current market price only.
7.6 ⚠️ Obligation: Disbursal of individual housing loans in projects must be linked to construction stages; upfront disbursal for incomplete/greenfield projects is prohibited.

### 8. Housing Loans under Priority Sector
⚠️ Obligation: 8.1 Classification must follow the Master Directions on Priority Sector Lending dated March 24, 2025.

### 9. Precautions
9.1 Banks must verify the genuineness of documents independently to avoid multiple financing frauds.
⚠️ Obligation: 9.2 Banks must ensure loans are not for unauthorized constructions or misuse of properties.
⚠️ Obligation: 9.3 In accordance with the Hon'ble Bombay High Court, banks must stipulate that builders disclose the mortgage charge in all advertisements and brochures and provide an NOC for the sale of flats.

### 10. National Building Code
⚠️ Obligation: Adherence to the NBC is advisable for safety against natural disasters and should be incorporated into bank loan policies.

## Definitions

- **Graduated Basis:** Fixing lower repayment instalments in the initial years and gradually increasing them in subsequent years.
- **CRE-RH (Commercial Real Estate – Residential Housing):** Loans to builders/developers for residential housing projects (excluding non-residential commercial real estate) where the commercial area does not exceed 10% of the total FSI.
- **IPRE (Income-producing real estate):** Funding where repayment and recovery depend primarily on the cash flows generated by the asset (e.g., lease/rental payments).
- **Small Value Loans:** Housing loans up to specific limits defined by UCB Tiering for individual residential units.
