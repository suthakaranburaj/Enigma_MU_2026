# Master Circular – Basel III Capital Regulations

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/08 (DOR.CAP.REC.2/21.06.201/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks (Excluding Small Finance Banks, Payments Banks, and Regional Rural Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Capital Adequacy
**Supersedes:** Master Circular No. DOR.CAP.REC.4/21.06.201/2024-25 dated April 01, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular provides a consolidated framework for Basel III capital adequacy standards for Scheduled Commercial Banks in India. It defines the minimum capital requirements (Pillar 1), the internal process for assessing capital adequacy (ICAAP/Pillar 2), and mandatory public disclosures (Pillar 3). Banks must maintain a Minimum Total Capital of 9% and a Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio of 5.5%, supplemented by a 2.5% Capital Conservation Buffer. The circular also mandates a non-risk-based Leverage Ratio and a Countercyclical Capital Buffer to ensure banking sector resilience during economic cycles.

## Sections

### Part A: Minimum Capital Requirement (Pillar 1)

#### 1. Introduction
The Basel III reforms seek to improve the banking sector’s ability to absorb shocks and reduce risk spillover to the real economy.

#### 2. Approach to Implementation and Effective Date
2.4 ⚠️ Obligation: The Basel III capital regulations were implemented in India with effect from April 1, 2013 and have been fully implemented as on October 1, 2021. Banks have to comply with the regulatory limits and minima as prescribed under Basel III capital regulations, on an ongoing basis.

#### 3. Scope of Application of Capital Adequacy Framework
3.1 ⚠️ Obligation: A bank shall comply with the capital adequacy ratio requirements at two levels: (a) consolidated (“Group”) level and (b) standalone (“Solo”) level.

#### 4. Composition of Regulatory Capital
4.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall maintain a minimum Pillar 1 Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR) of 9% on an on-going basis (other than capital conservation buffer and countercyclical capital buffer etc.).
4.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital must be at least 5.5% of risk-weighted assets (RWAs).
4.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Tier 1 capital must be at least 7% of RWAs on an ongoing basis.
4.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Total Capital (Tier 1 + Tier 2) must be at least 9% of RWAs on an ongoing basis.

#### 4.4 Regulatory Adjustments / Deductions
4.4.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Goodwill and all other intangible assets should be deducted from Common Equity Tier 1 capital.
4.4.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Deferred tax assets (DTAs) associated with accumulated losses shall be deducted in full from CET1 capital.

#### 5. Capital Charge for Credit Risk
5.2.1 Claims on the central government will attract a zero risk weight.
5.2.2 Direct loan/credit exposures to State Governments and investment in State Government securities will attract zero risk weight. State Government guaranteed claims attract 20% risk weight.

#### 5.9 Claims included in the Regulatory Retail Portfolios
5.9.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Claims that meet all four qualifying criteria (Orientation, Product, Granularity, and Low value) shall be assigned a risk weight of 75%.
5.9.3 (iv) ⚠️ Obligation: The maximum aggregated retail exposure to one counterpart should not exceed the absolute threshold limit of ₹ 7.5 crore.

#### 5.10 Claims secured by Residential Property
5.10.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Lending to individuals for residential property shall be risk weighted based on LTV ratios (e.g., LTV ≤ 80% attracts 35% risk weight for loans up to Rs. 30 lakh).

#### 5.12 Non-Performing Assets (NPAs)
5.12.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The unsecured portion of NPA shall be risk-weighted at 150% when specific provisions are less than 20% of the outstanding amount.

#### 5.13 Specified Categories
5.13.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Claims on Venture Capital Funds will attract a risk weight of 150%.
5.13.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Consumer credit exposure (excluding housing/education/vehicle/gold loans) will attract a risk weight of 125%.

#### 5.15 Off-Balance Sheet Items
5.15.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The total risk-weighted off-balance sheet credit exposure is calculated by converting notional amounts to credit equivalents using Credit Conversion Factors (CCFs).

#### 5.17 Credit Default Swap (CDS) Positions in the Banking Book
5.17.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: In case of Banking Book positions hedged by bought CDS, exposure is deemed substituted by the protection seller if specific operational requirements are met.

#### 6. External Credit Assessments
6.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should use the chosen credit rating agencies and their ratings consistently for each type of claim. Banks will not be allowed to “cherry pick” assessments.

#### 7. Credit Risk Mitigation (CRM)
7.1.2 ⚠️ Obligation: No transaction in which CRM techniques are used should receive a higher capital requirement than an otherwise identical transaction where such techniques are not used.
7.2 ⚠️ Obligation: All documentation used in collateralised transactions and guarantees must be binding on all parties and legally enforceable in all relevant jurisdictions.

#### 8. Capital Charge for Market Risk
8.2.1 Market risk is defined as the risk of losses in on-balance sheet and off-balance sheet positions arising from movements in market prices.
8.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are required to manage the market risks in their books on an ongoing basis and ensure that the capital requirements are maintained at the close of each business day.

#### 9. Capital Charge for Operational Risk
9.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks in India shall compute the capital requirements for operational risk under the Basic Indicator Approach (BIA).
9.3.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall hold capital for operational risk equal to the average over the previous three years of 15% of positive annual gross income.

### Part B: Supervisory Review and Evaluation Process (SREP)

#### 10. Introduction to the SREP under Pillar 2
10.6 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks were advised to develop and put in place, with the approval of their Boards, an ICAAP commensurate with their size, level of complexity, and risk profile.

#### 12. Guidelines for SREP of the RBI and the ICAAP of the Banks
12.3.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Every bank shall have an ICAAP. It shall be prepared on a solo basis at every tier and at the level of the consolidated bank.
12.3.3.6 ⚠️ Obligation: The ICAAP Document should reach the RBI latest by end of the first quarter (i.e., April-June) of the relevant financial year.

### Part C: Market Discipline (Pillar 3)

#### 14. Guidelines on Market Discipline
14.7 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should have a formal disclosure policy approved by the Board of Directors that addresses the bank’s approach for determining what disclosures it will make.
14.9.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are required to make Pillar 3 disclosures at least on a half-yearly basis (Quarterly for Tables DF-2, DF-3, and DF-4).

### Part D: Capital Conservation Buffer Framework

#### 15. Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB)
15.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are required to maintain a CCB of 2.5%, comprised of CET1 capital, above the regulatory minimum CRAR of 9%.
15.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Capital distribution constraints (on dividends, buybacks, and bonuses) will be imposed if CET1 falls within the buffer range (e.g., 100% constraint if CET1 is between 5.5% - 6.125%).

### Part E: Leverage Ratio Framework

#### 16. Leverage Ratio
16.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: The minimum Leverage Ratio shall be 4% for Domestic Systemically Important Banks (D-SIBs) and 3.5% for other banks.
16.5.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are required to publicly disclose their Basel III leverage ratio on a consolidated basis.

### Part F: Countercyclical Capital Buffer (CCCB) Framework

#### 17. Countercyclical Capital Buffer
17.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The CCCB may be maintained in the form of CET1 capital only, and the amount may vary from 0 to 2.5% of total RWAs.
17.2.6 ⚠️ Obligation: CCCB shall be maintained on a solo basis as well as on consolidated basis.

## Definitions

- **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1):** The highest quality component of capital, primarily common shares and retained earnings, ensuring bank solvency.
- **Additional Tier 1 (AT1):** Capital instruments like Perpetual Non-Cumulative Preference Shares (PNCPS) that absorb losses on a going-concern basis.
- **Tier 2 Capital:** Subordinated debt and general provisions that absorb losses in a "gone-concern" (liquidation) situation.
- **Credit Risk:** The risk that a party to a contractual agreement will be unable to meet their obligations.
- **Market Risk:** The risk of losses arising from movements in market prices (interest rates, equities, foreign exchange).
- **Operational Risk:** The risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people, and systems or from external events.
- **ICAAP:** Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process; a bank's own assessment of its risks and required capital.
- **SREP:** Supervisory Review and Evaluation Process; the RBI's evaluation of a bank's ICAAP.
- **LTV Ratio:** Loan-to-Value ratio; the loan amount as a percentage of the value of the mortgaged property.
- **Risk Weighted Assets (RWA):** Assets weighted according to their risk level to determine minimum capital requirements.
