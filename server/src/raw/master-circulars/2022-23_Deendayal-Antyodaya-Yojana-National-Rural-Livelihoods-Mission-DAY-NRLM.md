## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the guidelines for the implementation of the Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM). It serves as a comprehensive framework for banks to provide financial assistance to Self Help Groups (SHGs) of the rural poor, particularly women. The mission aims at poverty reduction through building strong institutions for the poor and enabling access to financial services. Key features include the provision of Revolving Funds, Community Investment Support Funds, and an Interest Subvention Scheme. It specifically outlines the credit linkage processes, loan amounts, repayment schedules, and security requirements, notably enhancing the collateral-free loan limit for SHGs.

## Sections

### 1. Background
The Swarnajayanti Gram Swarojgar Yojana (SGSY) was restructured into NRLM effective April 1, 2013, and renamed DAY-NRLM on March 29, 2016. It is a flagship program for poverty reduction through building institutions for the poor, specifically women.

### 2. Women SHGs and their Federations
2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: DAY-NRLM promotes affinity-based women SHGs. Groups for persons with disabilities, elders, and transgenders may include both men and women.
2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: SHGs consist of 10-20 members; special SHGs (remote/tribal/disabled) may have a minimum of 5 members.

### 3. Revolving Fund (RF)
⚠️ Obligation: MoRD provides RF support of ₹20,000 - ₹30,000 per SHG to groups in existence for 3/6 months that follow 'Panchasutras'.

### 4. Capital Subsidy
No capital subsidy is sanctioned to any SHG under DAY-NRLM.

### 5. Community Investment Support Fund (CIF)
CIF is routed through village/cluster federations to advance loans to SHGs for socio-economic activities.

### 6. Interest Subvention
Women SHGs are eligible for interest subvention. Salient features are in Annex II.

### 7. Role of Banks

#### 7.1 Opening of Savings/Current Accounts
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall open savings accounts for SHGs and their federations.
⚠️ Obligation: KYC verification for SHG members must adhere to the Master Direction on KYC.
⚠️ Obligation: Business Correspondents (BCs) may be authorized to open SHG accounts.
⚠️ Obligation: Opening of savings account of all members with the bank shall not be made a prerequisite for credit linkage.

#### 7.2 Transaction in Savings/Cash Credit account
⚠️ Obligation: Banks are advised to put in place dual-authentication facility in both ON-US and OFF-US environments for BC-managed retail outlets.

#### 7.3 Lending to SHGs and their individual members
7.3.1 ⚠️ Obligation: SHGs must be in active existence for at least 6 months and practicing 'Panchasutras'.
7.3.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must use Common Loan Application Forms devised by IBA.
7.3.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks are advised to sanction a minimum CCL of ₹6 lakh for 3 years.
7.3.3(iii) ⚠️ Obligation: Drawing Power (DP) for CCL:
- Year 1: 6 times corpus or min ₹1.5 lakh.
- Year 2: 8 times corpus or min ₹3 lakh.
- Year 3: Min ₹6 lakh based on Micro Credit Plan (MCP).
- Year 4+: Above ₹6 lakh based on MCP.
7.3.3(iv) ⚠️ Obligation: Term Loan (TL) doses:
- First dose: 6 times corpus or min ₹1.5 lakh.
- Second dose: 8 times corpus or min ₹3 lakh.
- Third dose: Min ₹6 lakh based on MCP.
- Fourth dose+: Above ₹6 lakh based on MCP.

#### 7.3.4 Credit facilities to SHG members
⚠️ Obligation: Banks may consider loans up to ₹10 lakh to individual members of matured SHGs for viable enterprises.
⚠️ Obligation: One woman in every SHG may be provided up to ₹1 lakh under the MUDRA scheme.
⚠️ Obligation: Provide minimum OD facility of ₹5000 to every woman SHG member with a PMJDY account.

#### 7.3.5 Purpose of loan and repayment
⚠️ Obligation: MCPs prepared by SHGs form the basis for determining the purpose.
⚠️ Obligation: At least 50% of loans >₹1L, 75% >₹4L, and 85% >₹6L should be used for income-generating productive purposes.
⚠️ Obligation: Repayment schedules for TL range from 24 months (1st dose) up to 84 months (4th dose+).

#### 7.3.6 Security and Margin
⚠️ Obligation: For loans up to ₹10.00 lakh, no collateral and no margin shall be obtained. No lien on savings accounts.
⚠️ Obligation: For loans above ₹10 lakh and up to ₹20 lakh, no collateral shall be obtained. Entire loan is eligible for CGFMU coverage.
⚠️ Obligation: For loans above ₹10 lakh and up to ₹20 lakh, a margin not exceeding 10% of the amount exceeding ₹10 lakh may be obtained.

#### 7.3.7 Dealing with Defaulters
⚠️ Obligation: Willful defaulters should not be financed. Non-willful defaulters should not be debarred; genuine defaults should follow restructuring norms.

#### 7.3.8 Documentation and follow-up
⚠️ Obligation: Loan passbooks in regional languages should be issued and updated with every transaction.

### 8. Recovery
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall prepare a list of defaulting SHGs every month and furnish it to BLBC and DCC meetings.

### 9. Credit Target Planning and Monitoring
⚠️ Obligation: SLBCs shall constitute a sub-committee on SHG Bank Linkage.
⚠️ Obligation: District-wise credit plans must be communicated to the DCC.

### 10. Financial Literacy
Banks shall coordinate with SRLMs to utilize Financial Literacy Community Resource Persons (FL-CRPs) for village camps.

### 11. Data Sharing
⚠️ Obligation: Banks may share data with DAY-NRLM/SRLMs in mutually agreed formats, ensuring specific and separate customer consent.

### 12. DAY-NRLM support to Banks
SRLMs provide strategic partnerships, financial literacy assistance, and 'Bank Mitras' for recovery follow-up.

## Definitions

- **Panchasutras:** The five norms of good SHGs: regular meetings, regular savings, regular internal lending, regular recoveries, and maintenance of proper books of accounts.
- **Corpus:** Inclusive of revolving funds, own savings, interest earned from on-lending, and other income sources.
- **Micro Credit Plan (MCP):** A document prepared by SHG members to prioritize and plan for credit requirements.
- **Drawing Power (DP):** The limit up to which an SHG can withdraw funds from its Cash Credit account.
- **Dual Authentication:** Transaction authenticated by two members of the SHG through Aadhaar and biometrics.

## Annex II: Interest Subvention Scheme for Women SHGs (2025-26)

- **Applicability:** Rural areas only.
- **Loans up to ₹3 lakh:** Concessional interest rate of 7% p.a. Banks subvented at 4.5% p.a.
- **Loans ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh:** Interest rate at 1-year MCLR or benchmark-based rate or 10% (whichever is lower). Banks subvented at 5% p.a.
- **Eligibility:** Standard accounts only.
- **Nodal Bank:** Indian Bank.

---

## Annex III: Women Enterprise Acceleration Fund

- **Reimbursement:** Credit Guarantee fees (CGTMSE/CGFMU) reimbursed for loans up to ₹5 lakh for max 5 years.
- **Interest Subvention:** 2% interest subvention for prompt repayment on outstanding up to ₹1.5 lakh per borrower (max 3 years).
