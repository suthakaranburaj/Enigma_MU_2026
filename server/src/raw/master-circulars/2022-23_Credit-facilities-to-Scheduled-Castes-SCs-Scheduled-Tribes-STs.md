# Master Circular - Credit facilities to Scheduled Castes (SCs) & Scheduled Tribes (STs)

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/56 (FIDD.CO.GSSD.BC.No.07/09.09.001/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 16 June 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks (including Small Finance Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** All circulars issued by Reserve Bank on the subject till date, as listed in the Appendix.

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates existing guidelines for banks to increase credit flow to Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs). It mandates that banks integrate SC/ST credit planning into the Lead Bank Scheme and Block-level plans, ensuring loan proposals are handled with sympathy. Banks are prohibited from insisting on deposits for government-sponsored poverty alleviation schemes and must ensure rejections are handled by a higher administrative level than the branch. The circular also outlines specific quotas under schemes like DAY-NRLM (50%) and the DRI Scheme (40%) to ensure equitable financial inclusion.

## Sections

### 1. Planning Process

#### 1.1 Co-ordination
The District Level Consultative Committees formed under the Lead Bank Scheme should continue to be the principal mechanism of co-ordination between banks and development agencies.
⚠️ Obligation: The district credit plans formulated by the Lead Banks should clearly indicate the linkage of credit with employment and development schemes.

#### 1.2 District Industries Centres
⚠️ Obligation: Banks will have to establish closer liaison with the District Industries Centres for promoting self-employment.

#### 1.3 Block Level Planning
⚠️ Obligation: At the block level, a certain weightage is to be given to SCs/STs in the planning process. Credit planning should be weighted in their favour and special bankable schemes suited to them should be drawn up.

#### 1.4 Review of Procedures
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should periodically review their lending procedures and policies to see that loans are sanctioned in time, are adequate and production-oriented.

#### 1.5 Target Localities
⚠️ Obligation: While formulating the Block/ District Credit Plan, special focus may be given to villages with sizeable population of SC/ST communities/ specific localities (bastis).

### 2. Role of Banks

#### 2.1 Technical Assistance
⚠️ Obligation: Bank staff may help the borrowers in filling up the forms and completing other formalities to ensure credit is received within a stipulated period.

#### 2.2 Awareness Creation
⚠️ Obligation: Awareness must be created through brochures, visits by field staff, etc. Banks should advise their branches to organize meetings more frequently exclusively for SC/ST beneficiaries.

#### 2.3 Internal Communication
⚠️ Obligation: Circulars issued by RBI/NABARD should be circulated among the staff for compliance.

#### 2.4 Deposits and Subsidies
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should not insist on deposits while considering loan applications under Government sponsored poverty alleviation schemes/self-employment programmes from SC/ST borrowers.
⚠️ Obligation: It should also be ensured that applicable subsidy is not held back while releasing the loan component till the full repayment of bank dues.

#### 2.5 Institutional Support
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should advise their branches/controlling offices to render institutional support to the National Scheduled Tribes Finance & Development Corporation and National Scheduled Castes Finance & Development Corporation.

#### 2.6 Priority Sector Classification
Loans sanctioned to State Sponsored Organisations for SCs/STs for purchase/supply of inputs and/or marketing of outputs are eligible for priority sector classification.

#### 2.7 Rejection Protocol
⚠️ Obligation: Rejection of SC/STs’ loan applications under government programmes should be done at the next higher level instead of at the branch level and reasons of rejection should be clearly indicated.

### 3. Role of SC/ST Development Corporations
Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribes Development Corporations can consider bankable schemes/proposals for bank finance.

### 4. Reservations for SC/ST beneficiaries under major Centrally Sponsored Schemes.

#### 4.1 DAY-NRLM
⚠️ Obligation: DAY-NRLM would ensure adequate coverage such that 50% of beneficiaries are SCs/STs.

#### 4.2 Differential Rate of Interest (DRI) Scheme
⚠️ Obligation: Banks have been advised to grant eligible borrowers belonging to SCs/STs advances to the extent of not less than 2/5th (40 percent) of total DRI advances.
Eligibility note: The criteria for size of land holding (1 acre irrigated/2.5 acres unirrigated) are not applicable to SCs/STs. They can avail of an additional housing loan up to ₹20,000/-.

### 5. Credit Enhancement Guarantee Scheme for Scheduled Castes (CEGSSC)
The CEGSSC was launched to promote entrepreneurship among SCs.
- **Nodal Agency:** IFCI Ltd.
- **Eligibility:** SC entrepreneurs/firms having more than 51% shareholding and management control for the previous 6 months.
- **Guarantee Cover:** ₹0.15 cr to ₹5.00 cr.
- **Tenure:** Maximum 7 years or repayment period, whichever is earlier.

### 6. Monitoring and Review

#### 6.1 Special Monitoring Cell
⚠️ Obligation: A special cell should be set up at the Head Office of banks for monitoring the flow of credit to SC/ST beneficiaries.

#### 6.2 Board Reporting
⚠️ Obligation: Any major gap or variation in credit flow to SCs/STs on a year to year basis should be reported to the Board as part of the review on the theme of “Financial Inclusion”.

#### 6.3 Quarterly Reviews
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should review the measures taken to enhance the flow of credit to SC/ST borrowers on a quarterly basis.

#### 6.4 SLBC Attendance
⚠️ Obligation: SLBC Convenor bank should invite the representative of National Commission for SCs/STs to attend SLBC meetings.

### 7. Reporting Requirements
⚠️ Obligation: Data on advances to SCs and STs should be reported as prescribed in the Master Direction on Priority Sector Lending.

## Definitions

- **SCs/STs:** Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes as recognized under the Constitution of India.
- **Lead Bank Scheme:** A scheme for coordinating activities of banks and other developmental agencies to deliver a coordinated approach to credit in rural areas.
- **DAY-NRLM:** Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Rural Livelihoods Mission.
- **DRI Scheme:** Differential Rate of Interest Scheme providing concessional credit at 4% p.a.
- **CEGSSC:** Credit Enhancement Guarantee Scheme for Scheduled Castes.
- **SCDC:** State Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Finance and Development Corporation.
