# Master Circular - Exposure Norms and Statutory / Other Restrictions - UCBs

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/19 DoR.CRE.REC.14/07.10.002/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Credit
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DoR.CRE.REC.71/07.10.002/2023-24 dated January 16, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates prudential instructions for Urban Co-operative Banks (UCBs) to manage credit risk and avoid concentration. It mandates exposure ceilings for individual and group borrowers linked to Tier-I capital, sets specific targets for "Small Value Loans" to promote financial inclusion, and establishes limits on real estate and unsecured advances. Furthermore, it details statutory and regulatory prohibitions, notably the ban on lending to directors, their relatives, or against the bank's own shares, ensuring robust governance and financial stability within the co-operative banking sector.

## Sections

### 1. General
1.1 As a prudential measure aimed at better risk management and avoidance of concentration of credit risk, UCBs have been advised to fix limits on their exposure to borrowers and specific sectors.
1.2 Banks must observe statutory and regulatory restrictions regarding advances against and investments in shares, debentures, and bonds.

### 2. Definitions
#### 2.1 Tier-I Capital
⚠️ Obligation: Tier-I capital as on March 31 of the preceding financial year shall be reckoned for fixing exposure limits. It shall be the same as prescribed for computation of capital adequacy.

#### 2.2 Exposure
Exposure includes both credit exposure (Loans and Advances) and investment exposure (Non-SLR securities).

#### 2.3 Credit Exposure
2.3.1 Includes funded/non-funded limits, equipment leasing, hire purchase financing, and ad hoc limits.
2.3.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Credit exposure shall not include loans and advances granted against the security of the bank's own term deposits.
2.3.3 The sanctioned limit or outstanding, whichever is higher, shall be reckoned.

#### 2.5 Group
⚠️ Obligation: The decision regarding the definition of a group is left to the UCB's perception based on commonality of management and effective control. Firms with common partners in the same line of business are deemed a connected group.

#### 2.6 Unsecured Advances
Includes clean overdrafts and loans against personal security but excludes advances backed by government guarantees, supply bills drawn on government, and the secured portion of partly secured advances.

### 3. Exposure Norms
#### 3.1 Exposure Ceiling to Individual / Group Borrowers
3.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: With Board approval, fix ceilings so that:
(i) Exposure to an individual borrower does not exceed 15% of Tier-I capital.
(ii) Exposure to a group of connected borrowers does not exceed 25% of Tier-I capital.

#### 3.3 Small value loans
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall have at least 50% of aggregate loans comprising Small Value Loans (value ≤ ₹25 lakh or 0.4% of Tier-I capital, whichever is higher), subject to a ceiling of ₹3 crore per borrower.
Glide Path for compliance:
*   March 31, 2025: 40%
*   March 31, 2026: 50%

#### 3.4 Real Estate Exposure Norms
3.4.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Aggregate exposure to residential mortgages (excluding priority sector) shall not exceed 25% of total loans and advances.
3.4.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Aggregate exposure to the real estate sector (excluding housing loans to individuals) shall not exceed 5% of total loans and advances.
3.4.6 ⚠️ Obligation: Housing loans to individuals are subject to ceilings based on UCB Tiers:
*   Tier 1: ₹60 lakh
*   Tier 2: ₹1.40 crore
*   Tier 3: ₹2 crore
*   Tier 4: ₹3 crore

### 4. Ceiling on Unsecured Advances
#### 4.1 Limits on unsecured advances
⚠️ Obligation: Individual/Group limits depend on CRAR and DTL:
*   CRAR ≥ 9%: Limits range from ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakh depending on DTL.
*   CRAR < 9%: Limits range from ₹0.25 lakh to ₹2 lakh depending on DTL.

#### 4.2 Aggregate Ceiling on Unsecured Advance
4.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Total unsecured advances should not exceed 10% of total assets as per the audited balance sheet of the preceding March 31.
4.2.2 UCBs with 90% priority sector portfolio and CRAR ≥ 9% may grant unsecured advances up to 35% of total assets with prior RBI approval.

### 5. Statutory Restrictions
5.2 ⚠️ Obligation: In terms of Section 20(1)(a) of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949, UCBs cannot grant loans and advances on the security of their own shares.
5.3 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall not, except with prior RBI approval, remit any debt due to it by past/present directors or firms in which they are interested.

### 6. Regulatory Restrictions
#### 6.1 Granting Loans and Advances to Directors and their Relatives
6.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs shall not make, provide, or renew any loans/advances to directors or their relatives, or to firms/companies in which they are interested. They shall not stand as surety/guarantor for such loans.
6.1.2 Exemptions: Employee-related loans to staff Directors, loans against Govt Securities/Fixed Deposits/Life Insurance in their own name.

#### 6.2 Maximum Ceiling on Advances to Nominal Members
⚠️ Obligation: Sanctioned for consumer durables/short term:
*   Deposits up to ₹50 crore: ₹50,000 per borrower.
*   Deposits above ₹50 crore: ₹1,00,000 per borrower.

#### 6.5 Bridge Loans / Interim Finance
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs are prohibited from entertaining any proposal for bridge loans/interim finance for all categories of NBFCs.

#### 6.6 Bank Finance against Shares, Debentures and Bonds
6.6.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Prohibited for stock brokers and commodity brokers.
6.6.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Individual loans limited to ₹5 lakh (physical) and ₹10 lakh (demat).
6.6.4 ⚠️ Obligation: Maintain a margin of 50%.
6.6.5 ⚠️ Obligation: Total aggregate of such loans shall not exceed 20% of Tier-I capital.

#### 6.8 Bank Finance to NBFCs
6.8.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should not finance NBFCs other than those engaged in hire-purchase/leasing. Prior approval from the Registrar of Co-operative Societies is needed for membership.
6.8.2 Limits for NBFC-ICC:
*   75% assets in leasing/HP: 3 times NOF.
*   Others: 2 times NOF.

#### 6.12 Restriction on Advances to Defaulters of Statutory Dues
6.12.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must satisfy themselves that there are no arrears of Provident Fund and other statutory dues by obtaining a declaration from borrowers.

## Definitions

- **Tier-I Capital:** Capital reckoned as of March 31 of the preceding financial year for exposure limit purposes.
- **Credit Exposure:** Sum of funded and non-funded credit limits, underwriting, and leasing/hire-purchase facilities.
- **Group Borrowers:** Borrowing units linked by commonality of management and effective control.
- **Unsecured Advance:** Loans without tangible security, excluding specific government-backed or high-liquidity instrument-backed advances.
- **Major Shareholder:** A person holding 10% or more of the paid-up share capital.
- **Relative:** Includes spouse, father, mother, son, daughter, and their respective spouses/step-relations as defined under the Companies Act.
