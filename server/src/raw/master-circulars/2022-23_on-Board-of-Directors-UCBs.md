Summary

This Master Circular consolidates and updates guidelines regarding the constitution, role, and responsibilities of the Board of Directors in Urban Co-operative Banks (UCBs). It mandates a minimum level of professionalism on the Board, requiring at least two directors with specific banking or professional expertise. For larger UCBs, the constitution of a separate Board of Management (BoM) is required. The circular strictly prohibits non-statutory honorary titles (e.g., Chairman Emeritus), limits donations to trusts where directors have interests, and defines a clear separation between policy oversight by the Board and day-to-day administration by the CEO/MD. Sections
1. Constitution of Board of Directors

1.1 Primary (urban) co-operative banks (UCBs or banks) function under the regulation and supervision of RBI under the BR Act, 1949 and RBI Act, 1934.

1.2 The directors on the boards of UCBs must be knowledgeable and persons of high integrity.
⚠️ Obligation: To ensure professionalism in the Board, banks should, at all times, have at least two professional directors, i.e., persons with suitable banking experience (at middle/senior management level) or with relevant professional qualification in the fields of law, accountancy or finance. Banks should also have a suitable provision in their byelaws to ensure this. (Not applicable to Salary Earners' Banks).

1.3 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs (other than those having deposit size less than Rs.100 crore and salary earners’ banks) are also required to constitute a Board of Management (BoM) in accordance with the guidelines contained in RBI circular dated December 31, 2019.

1.4 ⚠️ Obligation: Persons engaged in money lending, financing and investment activities (individual or via concern) or those convicted of criminal offences including moral turpitude are ineligible to be directors.

1.6 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs are directed not to create any honorary positions/titles at Board level or confer such titles that are non-statutory in nature (e.g., Chairman Emeritus, Group Chairman).

2. Role of directors

2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The Board of Directors (BOD or Board) is primarily concerned with formulation of policies and exercise of overall supervision and control. Day-to-day administration must be left to the Managing Director (MD) / Chief Executive Officer (CEO).

2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: It should be ensured that all circulars and other material relating to policies issued by the RBI are placed before the Board for information and appropriate action.

2.3 Directors of UCBs are advised to be guided by several guidelines:
i. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors should attend Board meetings regularly and study papers thoroughly. They should not directly call for papers/files/notes from various departments for scrutiny.
ii. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors are expected to ensure confidentiality of the Bank's agenda papers/notes and not reveal information relating to any constituent of the bank (under oath of secrecy).
iii. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors should not issue/give any instructions/direction to any individual officer/employee of the bank and should discourage employees/unions from approaching them.
vii. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors should not involve themselves in personnel administration (appointments, transfers, promotions) or redressal of individual grievances.
viii. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors should not approach or exert influence for the sanction of any facility or sponsor any loan proposal, site, or enlistment of contractors.
ix. ⚠️ Obligation: Directors should not participate in Board discussion if a proposal in which they are directly or indirectly interested comes up. They should disclose interest well in advance.
x. ⚠️ Obligation: Bank logos or distinctive designs should not be displayed on the directors' visiting cards or letterheads.

3. Committees of the Board
3.1 Audit Committee of the Board

3.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: An apex Audit Committee (ACB) should be set up at the Board level. It may consist of a chairman and three/four directors, with one or more being chartered accountants or having experience in management, finance, accountancy, and audit.

3.1.2 ⚠️ Obligation: The ACB should review RBI guideline implementation and submit a note to the Board quarterly. Major duties include:
i. Providing direction and oversight to the entire audit function.
ii. Reviewing internal inspection/audit function systems and follow-up (specifically inter-branch adjustments, book balancing, and frauds).
iii. Compliance with statutory/concurrent/RBI audit reports.
iv. Reviewing accounting policies for transparency and adequacy.

3.2 Risk Management Committee of the Board

⚠️ Obligation: UCBs having asset size of ₹5000 crore or above (as on March 31 of the previous year) are advised to set up a Risk Management Committee (of the Board).

4. Calendar of reviews – Matters to be placed before the Board of Directors

⚠️ Obligation: Directors should bestow attention on periodical reviews. An illustrative list with periodicity is provided in Annex 2.

5. Donations to trusts and institutions where directors or their relatives hold position or are interested

5.1 ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs are prohibited from giving donations to trusts and institutions where directors and/or their relatives hold a position or are interested, even within the 1% profit ceiling.

5.2 Relatives are defined as members of a Hindu Undivided Family, husband and wife, or specified relations (Father, Mother, Son, Son's wife, Daughter, Daughter's husband, Brother, Brother's wife, Sister, Sister's husband).

6. Payment of fees and allowances to directors

⚠️ Obligation: All expenses on Board meetings must be shown against P&L Account item 3 ("Directors and Local Committee Members – Fees and Allowance").

Definitions

Professional Directors: Directors having suitable banking experience at a middle/senior management level or relevant professional qualifications in law, accountancy, or finance.

Board of Management (BoM): A professional body constituted within the bank (for UCBs over Rs. 100 crore deposits) to oversee banking-related activities, separate from the primary BOD.

Relative: As defined in section 5.2, encompassing HUF members, spouses, parents, children, and siblings (including "step" relations).

Interest (in Trust): Positions held as trustees, beneficiaries, or involvement in the working of the trust likely to influence director independence.
