# Master Circular - Disbursement of Government Pension by Agency Banks

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/05 (CO.DGBA.GBD.No.S1/31.02.007/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Agency Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Payments
**Supersedes:** Master Circular RBI/2024-25/06 dated April 01, 2024

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates all instructions regarding the disbursement of Central and State Government pensions by agency banks. It requires banks to act immediately on government orders regarding Dearness Relief (DR) available on websites, eliminating manual delays. Banks must ensure proper facilities for sick or disabled pensioners and are mandated to compensate pensioners for any delays in disbursing revised pensions or arrears at a fixed interest rate of 8% per annum. The circular also standardizes the refund process for overpayments and emphasizes high-quality customer service through Nodal Officers and Centralized Pension Processing Centres (CPPCs).

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
Payment of pension to retired government employees, including basic pension and dearness relief (DR), is governed by schemes of concerned Ministries/Departments. This circular consolidates instructions till March 31, 2025.

### 2. Government orders on DR, etc. on websites
⚠️ Obligation: The procedure of forwarding related government orders in respect of dearness relief etc. to pension disbursing agency banks has been discontinued. Agency banks may act on the copies of government orders provided through post, fax, e-mails or by accessing from the government websites and authorize their pension paying branches to make payments to the pensioners immediately.

### 3. Prompt implementation of Government’s instructions
⚠️ Obligation: All agency banks are advised to scrupulously follow all the guidelines/ instructions contained in various notifications of Government (Central as well as States) and take necessary action immediately without waiting for any further instructions from RBI.

### 4. Timing of pension disbursement
⚠️ Obligation: The pension paying banks will credit the pension amount in the accounts of the pensioners based on the instructions given by respective Pension Paying Authorities.

### 5. Refund of excess pension payment to Government
⚠️ Obligation: Whenever any excess/overpayment is detected, the entire amount thereof should be credited to the Government account in lump sum immediately, when the excess/overpayment is due to an error on the part of the agency bank. This action is independent of recovery from the pensioner.

6. ⚠️ Obligation: If the excess/wrong payment is due to errors committed by the government, banks may take up the matter for quick resolution. This must be a time bound exercise and the government authority’s acknowledgement must be kept on the bank’s record.

### 7. Withdrawal of pension by old/ sick/ disabled/ incapacitated pensioners
Banks may categorize such pensioners as:
(a) Too ill to sign or be physically present.
(b) Physically incapacitated and unable to provide a thumb impression.

8. ⚠️ Obligation: For thumb/toe impressions, they must be identified by two independent witnesses known to the bank, one of whom should be a responsible bank official.
9. ⚠️ Obligation: If a pensioner mark is used instead of an impression, it must be identified by two independent witnesses, including a bank official from the same branch.

### 10. Implementation and Sensitization
⚠️ Obligation: Agency banks are requested to instruct their branches to display instructions on notice boards and sensitize staff regarding facilities for sick and disabled persons.

### 11. Reimbursement of pension payments
Link branches submit claims to RBI, Central Accounts Section, Nagpur (Central Govt) or Government Banking Division at Regional Offices (State Govt).

### 12. Continuation of joint accounts after death of pensioner
⚠️ Obligation: In case the spouse (family pensioner) opts for the existing joint account for credit of family pension, banks should not insist on opening a new account if the spouse is the survivor and authorization exists in the PPO.

### 13. Life Certificate - Issuance of Acknowledgement
⚠️ Obligation: Agency banks must mandatorily issue duly signed acknowledgements for life certificates submitted over the counter.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks should consider entering receipt in CBS to issue system-generated acknowledgements and provide digital acknowledgments for digital life certificates.

### 14. Single Window System
The objective is to make each pension paying bank responsible for settlement and reconciliation without manual intervention from RBI or SBI.

### 15. Customer Service
⚠️ Obligation: All agency banks may issue instructions to adhere to the recommendations of the Prabhakar Rao Committee. A checklist (Annex 1) should be provided to inspecting officers/auditors.

16. ⚠️ Obligation: Agency banks should appoint one/two nodal officers at each Region/Zone for monitoring grievance resolution. The GM/CGM concerned should review the position at monthly intervals.

17. ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should establish a toll-free dedicated pension line manned by trained persons with database access.

### 18. Compensation for Delay
⚠️ Obligation: Pension paying banks should compensate the pensioner for delay in crediting pension/arrears at a fixed interest rate of 8 per cent per annum for the delay after the due date of payment. This shall be credited automatically without any claim from the pensioner.

⚠️ Obligation: All branches having pension accounts should guide and assist the pensioners. Arithmetic details of calculations should be placed on the web or made available at branches.

## Definitions

No formal definitions section provided. Key operational terms used:
- **Agency Banks:** Banks authorized to conduct government business on behalf of RBI.
- **CPPC:** Centralized Pension Processing Centre.
- **DR:** Dearness Relief.
- **PPO:** Pension Payment Order.
