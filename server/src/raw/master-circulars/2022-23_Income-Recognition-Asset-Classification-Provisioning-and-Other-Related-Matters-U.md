# Master Circular - Income Recognition, Asset Classification, Provisioning and Other Related Matters - UCBs

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/14 (DOR.STR.REC.10/21.04.048/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks
**Regulatory Topic:** Asset Classification
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.STR.REC.9/21.04.048/2024-25 dated April 02, 2024

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the prudential framework for Urban Co-operative Banks (UCBs) concerning the identification and management of Non-Performing Assets (NPAs). It mandates an objective and uniform system for income recognition, asset classification, and provisioning based on record of recovery. Key features include the definition of "out of order" for overdrafts, specific timelines for classifying incipient stress through Special Mention Accounts (SMA), and staggered provisioning requirements for standard assets. The circular also provides detailed guidelines for restructuring advances and handling defaults in the agricultural sector.

## Sections

### 1. GENERAL
1.1 Reserve Bank has introduced prudential norms for income recognition, asset classification and provisioning for the advances portfolio.
1.2 Income recognition should be objective and based on record of recovery.
1.3 ⚠️ Obligation: The requirements of the State Co-operative Societies Acts and/or rules must be followed if they are more stringent than these norms.

### 2. NON-PERFORMING ASSETS (NPA)

#### 2.1 Classification of assets as Non-Performing
2.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: A loan or advance is an NPA where:
(i) Interest and/or installment of principal remain overdue for more than 90 days in respect of a Term Loan.
(ii) The account remains 'out of order' for 90 days in respect of an Overdraft/Cash Credit.
(iii) The bill remains overdue for more than 90 days in the case of bills purchased and discounted.
(iv) Agricultural advances: Overdue for two crop seasons (short duration) or one crop season (long duration).
(v) Any amount to be received remains overdue for more than 90 days in respect of other accounts.

#### 2.1.2 Credit Card Accounts
⚠️ Obligation: A credit card account is treated as NPA if the minimum amount due is not paid fully within 90 days from the payment due date.

#### 2.1.4 Identification of Assets as NPAs on ongoing basis
(i) ⚠️ Obligation: Identification of NPAs must be done on an on-going basis and provisions made at the end of each calendar quarter.
(ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Borrower accounts shall be flagged as overdue as part of day-end processes.

#### 2.1.6 Classification as Special Mention Account (SMA)
⚠️ Obligation: All UCBs shall classify loans as SMA:
- SMA-0: 0-30 days overdue.
- SMA-1: 31-60 days overdue.
- SMA-2: 61-90 days overdue.
(iii) ⚠️ Obligation: UCBs with total assets of ₹500 crore and above shall report SMA status to CRILC for borrowers with aggregate exposures of ₹5 crore and above.

### 2.2 Treatment of Accounts as NPAs
2.2.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Classification of NPAs must be done borrower-wise and not facility-wise.
2.2.5 ⚠️ Obligation: Credit facilities backed by Central Government guarantees though overdue should not be treated as NPA for provisioning (except for income recognition), but State Government guarantees attract normal norms if overdue >90 days.

#### 2.2.7 Prudential Guidelines on Restructuring of Advances
2.2.7.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Accounts classified as 'standard assets' should be immediately re-classified as 'sub-standard assets' upon restructuring.
2.2.7.11 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must provide for the diminution in the fair value of restructured advances.
2.2.7.20 ⚠️ Obligation: Total provisions required (normal + diminution) are capped at 100% of the outstanding debt.

### 3. ASSET CLASSIFICATION
3.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should classify assets into: Standard, Sub-standard, Doubtful, and Loss.
3.3.1 (ii) ⚠️ Obligation: Accounts with potential threats to recovery (erosion in security value >50%) should be straightaway classified as Doubtful or Loss assets.

### 4. INCOME RECOGNITION
4.1.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Income from NPA is not recognized on accrual basis but booked only when actually received.
4.2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: If an advance becomes NPA, interest accrued and credited to income account in the current and past periods, if uncollected, must be reversed.

### 5. PROVISIONING NORMS

#### 5.1 Norms for provisioning on Loans & Advances
5.1.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Provisioning requirements:
(i) Loss Assets: 100% should be provided for or written off.
(ii) Doubtful Assets: 100% for the unsecured portion. For the secured portion:
- Up to 1 year: 20%
- 1 to 3 years: 30%
- More than 3 years: 100% (effective April 1, 2010).
(iii) Sub-standard Assets: General provision of 10% on total outstanding.
(iv) Standard Assets:
- Agriculture/SME: 0.25%
- CRE: 1.00%
- CRE-RH: 0.75%
- Others: 0.40%

#### 5.3 Provisioning Pertaining to Fraud Accounts
5.3.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The entire amount due to the bank must be provided for over a period not exceeding four quarters commencing with the quarter in which the fraud was detected.
5.3.2 ⚠️ Obligation: If there is a delay in reporting the fraud to RBI, the entire provisioning is required to be made at once.

## Definitions

- **Non-Performing Asset (NPA):** A loan or an advance where interest and/or installment of principal remain overdue for a period of more than 90 days.
- **Out of Order:** An OD/CC account where the balance remains continuously in excess of the limit for 90 days, or where there are no/insufficient credits to cover interest for 90 days.
- **Overdue:** Any amount due to the bank under any credit facility if not paid by the due date.
- **Standard Asset:** An asset which does not disclose any problems and does not carry more than normal risk.
- **Sub-standard Asset:** An asset which has remained NPA for a period less than or equal to 12 months.
- **Doubtful Asset:** An asset which has remained in the sub-standard category for a period of 12 months.
- **Loss Asset:** An asset where loss has been identified by the bank/auditors/RBI but the amount has not been written off.
- **Specified Period (Restructuring):** A period of one year from the date when the first payment of interest or installment of principal falls due under the terms of the restructuring package.
