# Master Circular - Bank Finance to Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/15; DOR.CRE.REC.No.05/21.04.172/2025-26
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Scheduled Commercial Banks (excluding Regional Rural Banks)
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.CRE.REC.No.17/21.04.172/2024-25 dated April 24, 2024

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates the Reserve Bank of India’s regulatory policies regarding the financing of Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) by Scheduled Commercial Banks. It outlines eligibility criteria for financing registered and unregistered NBFCs, defines activities that are ineligible for bank credit (such as financing IPOs or unsecured loans by NBFCs), and sets strict prudential exposure ceilings. **Note: This document is explicitly marked as "Withdrawn" on every page, indicating it has been superseded or cancelled.**

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
The RBI regulates financial activities of NBFCs under Chapter III B of the RBI Act, 1934. All NBFCs and HFCs must be mandatorily registered.

#### 1.1 Terminology
Defines 'Current investments', 'Long term investments', 'NBFCs' (including HFCs), and 'Unsecured loans'.

#### 1.2 Background
Credit related matters have been progressively deregulated to allow greater operational freedom, though restrictions on sensitive activities remain.

### 2. Bank Finance to NBFCs Registered with RBI
2.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The ceiling on bank credit linked to Net Owned Fund (NOF) is withdrawn for statutorily registered NBFCs engaged in asset financing, loans, factoring, and investments.
2.2 Banks may extend finance against second-hand assets financed by NBFCs.
2.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must formulate a Board-approved loan policy subject to the condition that prohibited activities (para 4 and 6) are not financed.

### 3. Bank Finance to NBFCs not Requiring Registration
3. ⚠️ Obligation: For exempt categories, banks must make credit decisions based on the purpose of credit, asset quality, and borrower repayment capacity, while referring to Ministry of Corporate Affairs guidelines.

### 4. Activities not Eligible for Bank Credit
4.1 ⚠️ Obligation: The following NBFC activities are ineligible for bank credit:
- Bills discounted/rediscounted by NBFCs (except for commercial vehicles and two/three wheelers under specific conditions).
- Investments of NBFCs in any company via shares or debentures (except for Stock Broking Companies).
- Unsecured loans or inter-corporate deposits made by NBFCs.
- Loans and advances by NBFCs to their subsidiaries or group entities.
- Finance to NBFCs for further lending to individuals for IPO subscriptions or secondary market share purchases.

#### 4.2 Leased and Sub-Leased Assets
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not enter into lease agreements departmentally with equipment leasing companies or other NBFCs engaged in equipment leasing.

### 5. Bank Finance to Factoring Companies
5.1 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks may finance 'NBFC-Factors' and 'NBFC-ICCs' provided they are registered under the Factoring Regulation Act, 2011, and the credit is secured by hypothecation of receivables.
5.2 ⚠️ Obligation: NBFC-Factors must derive at least 50% of income from factoring and hold 50% of assets in receivables to be eligible.

### 6. Other Prohibition on Bank Finance to NBFCs
#### 6.1 Bridge loans / interim finance
⚠️ Obligation: Banks shall not grant bridge loans or interim finance against capital/debenture issues to any category of NBFC. This cannot be circumvented by using different nomenclatures.

#### 6.2 Advances against collateral security of shares to NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Shares and debentures cannot be accepted as collateral for secured loans granted to NBFC borrowers for any purpose.

#### 6.3 Restriction on guarantees for placement of funds with NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must comply with restrictions on issuing guarantees for fund placement with NBFCs or non-bank entities.

### 7. Prudential Ceilings for Exposure of Banks to NBFCs
7.2 ⚠️ Obligation: Single NBFC exposure is restricted to 20% of the eligible capital base (Tier I). Group exposure is restricted to 25% of Tier I capital.
7.3 ⚠️ Obligation: Single NBFC (predominantly Gold Loans - 50% or more assets) exposure is capped at 7.5% of capital funds (Tier I + II). This may increase to 12.5% if funds are on-lent to the infrastructure sector.
7.5 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks should have an internal sub-limit for aggregate exposure to all gold loan NBFCs.
7.7 ⚠️ Obligation: Banks must adhere to intra-group limits regarding transactions and exposures.

### 8. Restrictions Regarding Investments Made by Banks in Securities / Instruments Issued by NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must follow guidelines under the Master Direction on Classification, Valuation and Operation of Investment Portfolio regarding non-SLR securities.

### 9. Risk Weight for Bank credit to NBFCs
⚠️ Obligation: Bank exposure to NBFCs is subject to capital charge as per the Risk Weights prescribed in the Master Circular – Basel III Capital Regulations.

## Definitions

- **Current investments:** Investments in the balance sheet classified as 'current assets' intended to be held for less than one year.
- **Long term investments:** All types of investments other than those classified as 'current assets'.
- **NBFCs:** Non-Banking Financial Companies registered with the RBI, including Housing Finance Companies (HFCs) registered under the NHB Act.
- **Unsecured loans:** Loans not secured by any tangible asset.
