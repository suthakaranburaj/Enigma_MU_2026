# Master Circular - Management of Advances - UCBs

**Document Type:** Master Circular
**Reference Number:** RBI/2025-26/18 (DOR.CRE.REC.No.13/07.10.002/2025-26)
**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified
**Applicable To:** All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks (UCBs)
**Regulatory Topic:** Other
**Supersedes:** Master Circular DOR.CRE.REC.No.27/07.10.002/2023-24 dated July 25, 2023

---

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates all existing instructions and guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India up to March 31, 2025, regarding the management of advances by Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks. It outlines the requirements for Board-approved loan policies, assessment methodologies for working capital, credit administration protocols (including interest rate transparency and Key Fact Statements), and specific regulations for lending against gold, real estate, and to SHGs/JLGs. **Note: The provided document carries a "Withdrawn" watermark, indicating it may have been superseded or cancelled shortly after issuance.**

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
UCBs must lay down transparent policies for credit dispensation with board approval, keeping in view credit exposure norms and RBI guidelines. 
⚠️ Obligation: The loan policy shall be reviewed by the Board at least once in a financial year.

### 2. Working Capital Requirements
Assessment for borrowers (other than MSE) up to ₹1 crore and MSE up to ₹5 crore can be made on projected annual turnover.
⚠️ Obligation: Working capital requirement is to be assessed at 25% of projected turnover, with UCBs providing a minimum of 20% and the borrower contributing 5% as Net Working Capital (NWC).
⚠️ Obligation: For limits above these thresholds, UCBs may adopt turnover, cash budgeting, or other methods. Book-debt finance must not exceed 75% of limits for inland credit sales; the remaining 25% must be financed through bills.

### 3. Loan System for Delivery of Bank Credit
⚠️ Obligation: For borrowers with limits of ₹10 crore and above, the loan component should normally be 80%. UCBs must appropriately price the loan and cash credit components.
⚠️ Obligation: Ad hoc credit limits should only be released after the borrower has fully utilized existing limits. Release must be governed by a Board-approved policy and subject to concurrent/internal audit.

### 4. Credit Administration
#### 4.1 Rate of Interest
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must ensure interest rates are transparent, known to customers, and published in every branch. 
⚠️ Obligation: Total interest debited to an account must not exceed the principal amount for short-term advances to small and marginal farmers (5 acres or less).
⚠️ Obligation: Foreclosure charges/prepayment penalties are prohibited on all floating rate term loans to individual borrowers.

#### 4.3 Opening of Current Accounts
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must insist on a declaration that the account holder is not enjoying credit facilities with any other bank. If facilities exist, a "No Objection Certificate" must be obtained from the lending bank.

#### 4.5 Defaults in Payment of Statutory Dues
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must ensure borrowers pay provident fund and other statutory dues promptly. Banks must insist on audited financial statements from borrowers enjoying large limits.

#### 4.6 Sanction of Advances
⚠️ Obligation: The Board must delegate specific powers to functionaries. Transgressions must be reported to the Head Office immediately. Oral or telephonic sanctions are prohibited.

#### 4.7 Monitoring Operations in Loan Accounts
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs are responsible for ensuring the proper end-use of funds. 
⚠️ Obligation: For accounts showing signs of turning into NPAs, UCBs must resort to more frequent inspections and insist on the pledge of stock rather than hypothecation.

#### 4.10 Diversion and Siphoning of Funds
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must have a mechanism to monitor the end-use of funds. Diversion includes utilizing short-term funds for long-term purposes or deploying funds for activities other than those sanctioned.

#### 4.15 Penal Charges
⚠️ Obligation: Penalty for non-compliance with material terms shall be treated as ‘penal charges’ and shall not be levied in the form of ‘penal interest’. There shall be no capitalization of penal charges.
⚠️ Obligation: No penalty should be charged for loans up to ₹25,000 for priority sector borrowers.

### 5. Reporting / Exchange of Credit Information
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must exchange information about the conduct of borrowers' accounts with other banks at least at quarterly intervals for limits of ₹5 crore and above.
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must file records of equitable mortgages and other security interests with CERSAI on an ongoing basis.

### 6. Prudential Guidelines on Restructuring of Advances
⚠️ Obligation: Restructuring must preserve economic value and not be used for evergreening. 
⚠️ Obligation: No account shall be taken up for restructuring unless financial viability is established. Borrowers involved in frauds or malfeasance are ineligible.

### 7. Specific Lending Activities
#### 7.1 Bridge Loans / Interim Finance
⚠️ Obligation: The grant of bridge loans or interim finance to any company (including finance companies or for Euro issues) is totally prohibited.

#### 7.2 Real Estate
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must frame comprehensive prudential norms relating to ceilings on total real estate loans and exposure limits.

#### 7.5 Advances against pledge of Gold / Silver Ornaments
⚠️ Obligation: Under the bullet repayment scheme, loans against gold ornaments must not exceed ₹2 lakh (increased to ₹4 lakh for UCBs meeting specific PSL targets). 
⚠️ Obligation: Tenor must not exceed 12 months. LTV ratio of 75% must be maintained on the total outstanding (including interest).
⚠️ Obligation: Advances for the purchase of gold in any form (bullion, coins, etc.) are prohibited.

### 8. Discounting / Rediscounting of Bills
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must only purchase/discount bills under LCs for genuine commercial transactions of their constituent borrowers.
⚠️ Obligation: Accommodation bills must not be purchased or discounted.

### 9. Loans to Self Help Groups (SHGs) / Joint Liability Groups (JLGs)
⚠️ Obligation: Lending must be direct; intermediaries are not permitted. 
⚠️ Obligation: The maximum loan to SHGs should not exceed four times the group's savings (can be 10x for well-managed groups).

### 10. Relief Measures for Natural Calamities
⚠️ Obligation: UCBs must evolve a policy framework for providing relief (restructuring, fresh loans, consumption loans up to ₹5,000) in areas officially declared as affected by calamities.

## Definitions

- **Aggregate Deposits:** The sum of demand and time deposits.
- **Demand Liabilities:** Liabilities payable on demand, including current deposits, demand portions of savings deposits, margins against LC/guarantees, and unclaimed deposits.
- **Time Liabilities:** Liabilities payable otherwise than on demand, including fixed deposits, cash certificates, and gold deposits.
- **Projected Turnover:** Gross sales including excise duty.
- **Unsecured Exposure:** An exposure where the realizable value of the security is not more than 10%, ab-initio, of the outstanding exposure.
- **Small and Marginal Farmers:** Farmers with land holdings of 5 acres or less.
- **Microfinance Loan:** A collateral-free loan to a household having annual income up to ₹3,00,000.
