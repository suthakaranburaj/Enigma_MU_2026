**Original Issue Date:** 01 April 2025
**Updated As On:** Not specified

**Regulatory Topic:** Governance
**Supersedes:** Master Circular RBI/2024-25/07; CO.DGBA.GBD.No.S2/31-12-010/2024-2025 dated April 1, 2024

## Summary

This Master Circular consolidates instructions regarding the payment of agency commission to banks for handling government business on behalf of the Central and State Governments. It defines eligible and ineligible transactions, establishes the specific reporting protocols for GST and direct tax collections, and outlines the revised rates for various services (pensions, receipts, and turnover). The circular also mandates strict audit requirements and timelines for claiming commission to ensure accuracy and prevent wrong claims, which are subject to penal interest.

## Sections

### 1. Introduction
The Reserve Bank of India pays agency commission to banks appointed under Section 45 of the RBI Act, 1934, for government business handled by them.

### 2. Government transactions eligible for agency commission
Transactions relating to the following are eligible:
(a) Revenue receipts and payments for Central/State Governments.
(b) Pension payments for Central/State Governments.
(c) Other items specifically advised by RBI.
(d) Stamp duty collection, provided no charges are collected from the public and no remuneration is received from the State Government.

### 3. Government transactions not eligible for agency commission
⚠️ Obligation: Agency banks paying their own tax liabilities (TDS, Corporation Tax, etc.) must indicate these separately in the scroll and exclude them from commission claims.
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must furnish a certificate confirming that own tax liabilities have been excluded from claims.

Activities ineligible for commission:
(a) Bank guarantees/security deposits for government contractors.
(b) Banking business of autonomous bodies, municipalities, or local bodies.
(c) Payments classified as capital in nature to cover losses of autonomous bodies.
(d) Prefunded schemes.
(e) Gold Monetisation Scheme, 2015.
(f) Letters of Credit / Bank Guarantees opened on behalf of Ministries.

### 4. Reporting of transactions by agency banks to RBI
⚠️ Obligation: Banks must upload luggage files for GST, Customs, and Direct Taxes (TIN 2.0) into QPX/e-Kuber on all days except Global holidays before 1800 hours.
⚠️ Obligation: State Government transactions (electronic and physical) must be transferred by the 5th of the following month.

### 5. Rates for agency commission
The rates applicable from July 1, 2019:
*   Receipts - Physical mode: ₹40 per transaction.
*   Receipts - e-mode: ₹9 per transaction.
*   Pension Payments: ₹75 per transaction.
*   Payments other than Pension: 6.5 paise per ₹100 turnover.

⚠️ Obligation: The rate of ₹75 per pension transaction is only applicable when the bank performs the entire work, including pension calculation. If the bank only credits the amount based on Government instructions, the turnover rate (6.5 paise per ₹100) applies.
⚠️ Obligation: Transactions eligible for pension commission shall not exceed 14 per pensioner per year.

### 6. Claiming agency commission
⚠️ Obligation: Claims must be submitted directly to CAS Nagpur (Central Govt) or respective Regional Offices (State Govt).
⚠️ Obligation: GST (currently 18%) shall be paid along with the agency commission.
⚠️ Obligation: Claims must be submitted within 60 calendar days from the end of the quarter.

### 7. Penal interest for wrong claims
⚠️ Obligation: Agency banks are liable to pay penal interest for settling wrong claims.
*   **Penalty Rate:** Bank Rate as notified by RBI plus 2%.

## Definitions

- **Agency Banks:** Banks appointed under Section 45 of the RBI Act, 1934, to carry out general banking business of Central and State Governments.
- **Receipts-e-mode:** Transactions involving remittance of funds via Internet banking or those not involving physical receipt of cash/instruments.
- **Luggage Files:** Data files containing government transaction records uploaded to RBI's e-Kuber system.
- **TIN 2.0:** The upgraded Tax Information Network channel for Direct Tax collections.
- **ICEGATE:** The Indian Customs Electronic Data Interchange Gateway for indirect tax collection.
